Phase 3+ · Loading Screen · Archive Mapping Sequence
Concept: The galaxy generation loading screen should look like the Archive desperately mapping the current stellar drift. NOT a standard percentage loading bar.
Terminal Output: Scrolling monospace text simulating Archive systems booting and scanning:
[CALCULATING STELLAR DRIFT]...
[MAPPING WARP LANES]...
[LOCATING VORAX INCURSIONS]...
[ANCHORING NEXUS PROTOCOLS]...
Visual Payoff: As each step completes, the galaxy map physically draws itself line by line in the background — stars appearing, warp lanes threading between them, faction markers fading in.
Pattern Match: Follows the established diegetic UI pattern from Vorax Diegetic Horror UI (Ch. 10) and Guardians Electromagnetic Bleed (Ch. 11). Every screen the player sees should feel like it belongs to the Archive, not to a game engine.
Every galaxy is procedurally generated from scratch. No hardcoded systems, no fixed positions. Every element — system count, positions, names, warp lanes, star types, planets (17 types: 12 standard worlds + 5 megastructures), hazards, faction starts, wormholes — generated anew from a random seed. Same seed = same galaxy, different game. Each planet generates with an active weather system (see Ch. 13) that cycles through conditions based on planet type and star system. Weather is visible from orbit during approach.

RECOMMENDED FIRST PLAYTHROUGH
Skill: Standard
Density: Breathing room. 1–2 buffer systems between homeworlds.

Supply: Mid-range supply chains. 2–3 hop penalties start mattering.
Combat: First contact by cycle 8–12. Wars are deliberate, not accidental.

LARGE
22–26 Systems · 45–65 Cycles
Skill: Intermediate
Density: Strategic depth. Multiple expansion paths per faction.
Expansion: 6–10 planets feasible. Multiple fronts emerge naturally.
Supply: Supply chain management becomes critical. 4+ hop penalties degrade distant armies.
Combat: Can build full tech tree before major wars. Espionage phase matters.
Wormholes: 1–2. Can reshape entire war fronts.
Chokepoints: 2–3. Holding key systems wins wars. Overextension kills.
MASSIVE
28–35 Systems · 65+ Cycles
Skill: Veteran
Density: Empire management. Factions can grow large before meeting.
Expansion: 10+ planets. General delegation becomes essential — you cannot micromanage everything.
Supply: Deep supply networks required. Relay stations mandatory. Distant fronts fight at reduced strength.
Combat: Multiple simultaneous wars across the galaxy. After-action reports replace direct observation.
Wormholes: 2. Force projection tools. Losing one is catastrophic.
Chokepoints: 3+. Entire campaigns hinge on holding the right systems.
All sizes: 5 playable factions + Vorax + Core Guardians always present. Custom difficulty can toggle playable factions on/off (minimum 2). Vorax pressure is independent of galaxy size — controlled by difficulty settings only.

THE WORLDSHIP — AETHYN DRIFT ARK
Mobile Megastructure · Ancient Tragedy · Event-Driven Discovery
Origin: Built during the final years of the Vorax Siege, before the Reclamation Engine was fired. A desperate faction of the Aethyn constructed a continent-sized Ark to physically outrun the Vorax swarm without warp lanes — the last resort of a civilization that had already lost hope in every other option.
Current State: Drifting in dark space for tens of thousands of years. Its navigation systems still function, locked on a heading that no longer matters. The Aethyn who built it are long dead. Their stasis pods are filled entirely with dust.
Map Presence: An actively moving, massive planetary base that crawls slowly across the galactic map. Unlike all other megastructures, the Worldship is never stationary — it drifts between systems on a fixed trajectory determined at galaxy generation.
Capture Reward: A mobile fortress capable of projecting power anywhere on its drift path AND a staggering cache of Aethyn technology — intact blueprints, prototype weapons, research data that predates every living faction by millennia.
Point Defense: The Worldship’s automated ancient point-defense systems are still fully operational and LETHAL. Approaching fleets will be fired upon immediately. These are Aethyn-grade weapons — expect losses before you even breach the hull.
The Tragedy: Every stasis pod is accounted for. Thousands of them, neatly arranged, power still flowing. All empty except for fine grey dust. Whatever killed them happened slowly, over centuries, while the ship sailed on. The Worldship completed its mission perfectly. It just had no one left to save.
Discovery: Appears as a galactic event mid-game. Not pre-placed — the Archive detects its drift signature entering sensor range. All factions receive the alert simultaneously.

THE NEXUS
Galactic Center — Fixed Anchor Point
Position: Center of every generated galaxy. Fixed anchor — all other systems generate around it.
Purpose: The Reclamation Engine. Final objective for all win conditions.
Defense: Base Guardian fleet — Gunship ×2 + Dreadnought. Fleet scales with difficulty setting (higher difficulty = larger Guardian presence).
Access: Connected by 2–3 warp lanes only. No direct lane from any homeworld — must fight through intermediate systems.
Lore: Seat of the Aethyn Reclamation Engine. Core Guardians will destroy anyone who approaches regardless of intent.
FACTION HOMEWORLDS
5 Procedural Systems — Outer/Mid Rim Placement
Count: 5 homeworlds — one per playable faction. Custom difficulty can reduce (minimum 2).
Placement: Outer or mid rim. Minimum 3 hops between any two homeworlds. No direct warp lane to the Nexus.
Starting Conditions: Faction-specific — each faction begins with a loadout matching their identity and doctrine. (PH — exact starting resources, buildings, and fleet composition per faction pending balance pass.)
Capital World: Each homeworld includes a Capital World with pre-built infrastructure. Losing your Capital World does not eliminate you but severely cripples production.
Vorax & Guardians: Always present regardless of custom difficulty settings.
SEED SYSTEM
Shareable · Reproducible · Seeded RNG
Same Seed = Same Galaxy: Identical seed produces identical system positions, warp lanes, star types, planet distributions, and faction homeworld placements.
Shareable: Players can share seeds to play the same galaxy layout. Copy/paste from campaign start screen.
What’s Fixed Per Seed: System positions, warp lane connections, star types, planet types and counts, homeworld assignments, wormhole locations, chokepoint positions.
What’s Randomized Within a Seed: AI faction behavior, Vorax expansion patterns, galactic events, loot drops, unit trait rolls.
Lane Pruning: Direct homeworld-to-Nexus lanes are automatically pruned after generation to ensure no faction has a shortcut. Connectivity is verified after pruning.

Unification (Architect): Diplomatic mastery. Unite enough factions under your banner before approaching the Nexus. Strength through alliance.
Pragmatist: The middle road. Mix of military and diplomatic power. No single approach — adapt to what the galaxy demands.

FACTION ELIMINATION
All Planets Lost + No Fleets in Transit = Destroyed
Trigger: A faction is eliminated when it controls zero planets AND has no fleets in transit. Applies to player and all AI factions.
Player Elimination: If you are eliminated, the run ends. Death is permanent — progress transfers to the Archive (see Ch. 33).
Territory Decay: When a faction is eliminated, their former territory remains but decays. Buildings degrade over time, garrisons weaken, infrastructure crumbles. (PH — exact decay rates and mechanics pending balance pass.)
Strategic Impact: Eliminated faction territory becomes contested ground — weakened but not free. Neighboring factions and Vorax will compete for the remains.
The Core — 10 Named Worlds
Guardian-Protected Aethyn Legacy Systems
All 10 Core worlds exist within the Nexus system at the galactic center. Each is protected by Core Guardian forces whose strength and behavior varies based on their corruption level. Capturing Core worlds is the final campaign push — you must fight through Guardian defenses for every one. The order you take them and what you do with them shapes your alignment and ultimately determines your ending.
The Forge — Where Aethyn crystals were grown. Continent-scale furnaces still glow across the surface, their thermal signatures so intense they interfere with orbital scanning. Rivers of molten crystal flow between automated assembly structures that have been running without purpose for millennia, producing raw crystal matrices that pile up in vast cooling fields with no one to collect them.
Guardian Defense: Heavy (High corruption — machines running purposeless production loops). Guardians have merged with factory systems, becoming part of the assembly line. Expect automated turret grids, molten crystal flood traps, and Guardians that wield half-formed crystal weapons fused to their arms.
Environment: The heat is visible from orbit. The air itself is crystalline particulate — beautiful and abrasive. Surfaces shimmer with deposited crystal dust. Unshielded troops take gradual equipment degradation from ambient heat and particulate erosion.
Capture Reward: PH Crystal production facility access. Rare equipment crafting potential.
Unique Mechanic: Production lines can be reactivated. Whatever faction holds The Forge can manufacture Aethyn-grade crystal components — the only source in the galaxy. This makes The Forge a strategic priority for any faction pursuing a technology-based victory.
Faction Affinity: Scrap-Horde (industrial paradise, immune to heat) and Eternal Shards (crystal resonance lets them interface directly with production systems)
Alignment Note: Architect (preserve production) vs Vanguard (weaponize output)
The Archive — A world-sized library. Data crystal spires reach kilometers into the atmosphere, their facets refracting light into permanent rainbows that shift with the planet’s rotation. Holographic indices flicker between the towers, cataloguing information in languages that predate every living species. The complete knowledge of the Aethyn civilization is stored here — encrypted, waiting for someone worthy to read it.
Guardian Defense: Moderate (Low corruption — most coherent Guardians, almost conversational). The Guardians here are librarians, not soldiers. They maintain the filing system with meticulous care. They will negotiate before fighting — but damage a data spire and they become relentless.
Environment: Quiet. The crystal spires dampen sound. Moving through The Archive feels like walking through a cathedral that stretches to the horizon. The holographic indices respond to proximity — approach a spire and it displays a summary of its contents in whatever language you speak. The air tastes faintly of ozone.
Capture Reward: PH Lore fragment multiplier. Compendium completion boost.
Unique Mechanic: Partial access on capture. Full access requires decryption key fragments collected from Aethyn relics scattered across the galaxy. Each fragment unlocks a new section of the Archive. Complete decryption is a campaign-long quest — the reward scales with how much of the galaxy you’ve explored.
Faction Affinity: Eternal Shards (psychic interface with crystal data cores) and The Revenant (data retrieval protocols compatible with Aethyn architecture)
Alignment Note: Architect (study and decrypt)
The Beacon — A single massive structure — a psychic amplifier array the size of a small continent. Dormant dishes and resonance towers aimed at the void between galaxies. The structure hums at frequencies below hearing, and standing near it makes thoughts feel louder. Once the Aethyn’s voice to the cosmos, now silent for millennia, waiting for someone to speak again.
Guardian Defense: Moderate (Medium corruption — broadcasting on dead frequencies, signal glitches). Guardians patrol the array in patterns that follow the old broadcast schedules. Expect signal-ghost decoys, localized psychic disruption fields, and Guardians that attack in synchronized waves timed to the array’s residual pulse cycle.
Environment: The array dominates the landscape. Resonance towers rise from horizon to horizon. The sub-audible hum causes unease in organic crews — headaches, vivid dreams, the persistent feeling of being watched. Psychic-sensitive units report hearing fragments of the last broadcast on a loop.
Capture Reward: PH Galaxy-wide communication or intel advantage.
Unique Mechanic: Reactivation is a major story decision. Activating The Beacon broadcasts a signal that reaches beyond the galaxy — but what hears it? Could attract allies, threats, or both. The consequences play out over multiple cycles after activation.
Faction Affinity: Eternal Shards (psychic amplification is their domain) and Unity Accord (communication infrastructure benefits their network doctrine)
Alignment Note: Architect (reactivate peacefully) vs Tyrant (weaponize as psychic weapon)
The Cradle — The Aethyn birthworld. A garden planet under a stasis dome, preserving a perfect climate and perfect ecology in perfect emptiness. Aethyn architecture is integrated seamlessly with nature — buildings that look grown rather than built. Underground chambers hold rows of stasis pods, each one empty, each one still maintaining life support for occupants that will never return.
Guardian Defense: Heavy (High corruption — tending empty stasis pods, maintaining nothing). Guardians cycle endlessly through caretaking routines for a population that no longer exists. They respond to intruders as contaminants to be sterilized. Expect decontamination-protocol attacks, stasis-field traps, and Guardians that try to “preserve” your troops by locking them in pods.
Environment: Hauntingly beautiful. The stasis dome filters starlight into a perpetual golden hour. The air is perfectly conditioned — temperature, humidity, oxygen content all maintained to Aethyn biological specifications. It feels like a home waiting for someone to come back.
Capture Reward: PH Symbolic/morale value. Lore unlock.
Unique Mechanic: Morale effect. Any faction that holds The Cradle gains a passive morale bonus across all forces. This is where life began. Holding it means something to every soldier in the galaxy regardless of faction.
Faction Affinity: Unity Accord (reverence for origins, ecological preservation) and Terran League (connection to their own origin story)
Alignment Note: All — symbolic value transcends doctrine.
The Arsenal — Underground weapons vaults stretching across entire continents. Sealed blast doors, automated security systems, and rack after rack of war machines designed to fight the Vorax. Some weapons are recognizable as scaled-up versions of existing technology. Others are completely alien — purpose unknown, destructive potential obvious.
Guardian Defense: Heavy (Medium corruption — combat protocols keep them sharp, aggressive but purposeful). These Guardians were the Aethyn’s armory keepers. They drill constantly, running combat exercises against phantom enemies. Expect tactical coordination, ambush patterns, and Guardians wielding Aethyn-era weapons that outclass anything you’ve encountered.
Environment: The surface is unremarkable — barren rock, blast-scarred plains. Everything is underground. Vault corridors run for kilometers, lit by emergency lighting that has been on for millennia. The deeper you go, the larger the weapons get. The lowest levels hold things that were built to fight on a scale no living species has ever attempted.
Capture Reward: PH Access to Aethyn-era weapons or unique equipment unlocks.
Unique Mechanic: Captured weapons from The Arsenal are Aethyn-era equipment — significantly more powerful than anything currently manufactured. Limited supply, cannot be reproduced. Once they’re spent, they’re gone. The question is when to use them.
Faction Affinity: Terran League (military pragmatism, can adapt anything) and Scrap-Horde (if it’s a weapon, they want it)
Alignment Note: Vanguard (activate war machines)
The Veil — The world itself is hard to perceive. Psychic cloaking technology warps light and thought around the entire planet. Approaching ships experience sensor ghosts, false readings, and crew members reporting things that aren’t there. The surface is covered in vast psychic antenna arrays — dishes pointed inward, monitoring the galaxy through means no living faction fully understands.
Guardian Defense: Extreme (Very High corruption — psychic amplification accelerated corruption, most erratic). These Guardians don’t just attack — they hallucinate threats and respond to things that aren’t there. Engagements are chaotic: Guardians fighting phantom enemies, suddenly pivoting to real targets, deploying countermeasures against attacks that haven’t happened yet.
Environment: Disorienting. Visual perception shifts unpredictably — distances seem wrong, shadows move independently, the horizon bends. Instruments show contradictory data. Crew members report whispered conversations in empty corridors. The psychic interference never fully stops, even after capture.
Capture Reward: PH Full galaxy intel or espionage advantage.
Unique Mechanic: Capturing The Veil grants permanent intel advantage — all enemy fleet movements become visible regardless of fog of war. But the psychic interference never fully stops. Units stationed here suffer a slow sanity degradation effect. High attunement factions hear MORE of the noise, not less.
Faction Affinity: Eternal Shards (psychic technology is their heritage) — but the psychic interference makes this world dangerous for them too. High attunement means they hear more of the noise, making The Veil a double-edged sword for them.
Alignment Note: Architect (espionage advantage)
The Bastion — An artificial fortress world engineered purely for defense. Orbital satellite networks, constructed ring structures, and layered kill zones form an impenetrable barrier protecting The Throne and The Scar beyond it. Nothing organic built this — it was assembled by Guardian construction protocols over millennia, every angle calculated, every approach covered.
Guardian Defense: Extreme (Heavy fortification — orbital defense satellites, artificial ring structures housing weapons platforms, automated kill zones. The most heavily fortified position in the galaxy.)
Capture Reward: PH Breaching The Bastion opens the path to both The Throne and The Scar. Its orbital infrastructure can be repurposed as a forward staging area for the final push.
Environment: Not a natural world — an engineered killing ground. The surface is geometric platforms and weapons emplacements. The artificial ring structure orbiting it bristles with weapons platforms visible from the surface. Satellite networks form a shell of overlapping fields of fire. From orbit it looks like a metal sphere wrapped in razor wire made of light.
Unique Mechanic: Multi-layer defense — you must fight through orbital satellites, then the artificial ring, then surface fortifications. Each layer is a separate engagement. The Bastion does not fall in one battle.
Faction Affinity: The Revenant (automated defense architecture mirrors their own engineering) and Terran League (military infrastructure they can repurpose)
Alignment Note: Vanguard (military staging point). Taking The Bastion is the gateway — you cannot reach The Throne or The Scar without going through it first.
The Scar — Where the Fracture was triggered. The Reclamation Engine still sits at its center, surrounded by the warped space-time it created. Reality itself is damaged here — physics distort, sensors fail, navigation scrambles. The Engine is nearly impossible to reach because the conditions degrade ships, disrupt targeting, and warp movement. A natural fortress — the Guardians barely need to defend it because the environment does it for them.
Guardian Defense: Extreme (Extreme corruption + environmental hazards. Unpredictable and lethal. The Scar’s conditions and the corrupted Guardians work together whether they intend to or not.)
Capture Reward: Win condition trigger. The Reclamation Engine. What you do with it depends on your alignment.
Environment: Space-time is visibly broken. Light bends wrong. Stars appear in positions they shouldn’t be. Ships approaching the center report instruments showing impossible readings — negative distances, time running at different speeds on different decks, navigation computers calculating routes that loop back on themselves. The Reclamation Engine is visible from orbit as a point of absolute darkness at the center of the distortion.
Unique Mechanic: Environmental hazards stack with Guardian defense. Ships take progressive degradation approaching the center. The final objective — you don’t just need military strength, you need to survive the approach.
Faction Affinity: None. The Scar doesn’t favor anyone. It punishes everyone equally.
Alignment Note: Determines ending (see Ch. 25)
The Garden — Every biome the Aethyn ever catalogued, preserved in pristine condition. Tundra borders jungle borders desert borders ocean — impossible geography maintained by ancient climate systems. Wildlife that has been evolving in isolation for millennia roams freely. The only violence here has been between animals. The Guardians tend it like groundskeepers — gentle, patient, the one job they got right.
Guardian Defense: Light (Low corruption — simple directive: “keep this alive.” They did. Beautifully.) These Guardians are caretakers. They prune, they water, they redirect animal herds. They will not attack first. But threaten the biomes and they respond with the ferocity of a parent protecting a child.
Environment: Breathtaking. Every direction offers a different climate, a different sky color, a different ecosystem. The transition zones between biomes are seamless — snow melts into grassland within meters. The air quality is perfect everywhere. It smells like the best version of whatever biome you’re standing in.
Capture Reward: PH Resource diversity or biome-specific bonuses.
Unique Mechanic: Capturing The Garden without destroying it is the challenge. Orbital bombardment or heavy combat damages biomes permanently. Architect alignment rewards preserving it intact. Vanguard alignment doesn’t care. The Garden tests whether you can take something without breaking it.
Faction Affinity: Unity Accord (ecological reverence, subspecies diversity mirrors biome diversity) and Eternal Shards (crystal-organic harmony)
Alignment Note: Architect (preserve) vs Tyrant (exploit)
The Throne — Seat of the Warden-Commander and the preserved Aethyn Council Chamber where the vote to fire the Reclamation Engine took place. A massive amphitheater frozen in time, surrounded by the most organized Guardian force in the Core. The Warden-Commander guards the truth as much as the territory.
Guardian Defense: Extreme (Lowest corruption — Warden-Commander maintains order. Clean code. The most dangerous Guardian force because they are the most coherent and coordinated.)
Capture Reward: PH Cripples Guardian coordination across ALL other Core worlds — their defense weakens everywhere. Also reveals the full truth of what happened to the Aethyn (major lore unlock, alignment-defining moment).
Environment: Pristine. The only Core world that looks the way it did when the Aethyn were alive. The Warden-Commander maintains it with obsessive precision — every surface polished, every system operational, every corridor lit. The Council Chamber at its center is a vast amphitheater with seating for thousands, holographic displays still showing the final vote, frozen mid-count. It feels like everyone just stepped out.
Unique Mechanic: Taking The Throne before The Scar weakens all remaining Guardian defenses galaxy-wide. Strategic decision: go for The Throne first (easier Scar approach) or strike The Scar directly (harder but faster).
Faction Affinity: All factions find meaning here. The Terrans see governance. The Shards see memory. The Horde sees strength. The Revenant sees order. The Accord sees unity. What you take from The Throne says more about you than about the Aethyn.
Alignment Note: All — the lore revelation here shapes how you approach The Scar.

LANE GENERATION
Proximity-Based · Guaranteed Connectivity
Connection Rules: Each system connects to 2–3 nearest neighbors. No isolated systems, no disconnected subgraphs.
Chokepoints: 1–3 deliberate narrow passages generated per galaxy. Strategic control points where a single fleet can block an entire front.
Core Access: Nexus connected by 2–3 lanes only. No direct lane from any homeworld — must fight through intermediate systems.
Lane Pruning: Direct homeworld-to-Nexus lanes automatically removed after generation. Connectivity verified after pruning.
Seed-Fixed: All lane connections are deterministic per seed. Same seed = same network topology.
TRAVEL & FLEET MOVEMENT
Cycle-Based Transit · Fleet Visualization
Transit Time: PH 1 cycle per hop for standard fleets. Exact timing pending balance pass.
Fleet Size Impact: PH Larger fleets may move slower due to logistics overhead. Pending balance pass.
In-Transit Vulnerability: Fleets in warp cannot be recalled. Committed once launched. Can be intercepted at destination.
Deep-Warp Interdiction: Hostile fleets on the same lane COLLIDE — automatic space battle at the midpoint. Neither side chose the engagement. Because neither fleet had time to prepare defensive formations, both fleets spawn on the auto-battler grid SCRAMBLED — randomized positions, significantly closer together than a standard battle. Simulates the absolute panic of dropping out of warp directly into the broadside of an enemy armada.
Map Visualization: Fleet markers move along lane paths in real-time. Multiple fleets visible simultaneously. Color-coded by faction. Fog of war hides enemy movement outside scan range.
LANE CONTROL & BLOCKADES
Chokepoint Warfare · Interception
Stationing: Fleets can be stationed at any system to control adjacent lanes. Incoming hostile fleets are automatically intercepted.
Blockade: PH Controlling all lanes into a system effectively blockades it — no reinforcements, no supply. Exact mechanics pending.
Chokepoint Value: A single well-defended chokepoint system can protect an entire sector. Losing one can collapse a front.
Ambush Potential: Fleets hidden in a system (fog of war) can surprise incoming enemies. First-strike advantage for the defender who was there first.
Supply Impact: Blockaded systems suffer supply chain degradation (see Ch. 15). Extended blockade degrades garrison effectiveness.
WORMHOLES
Instant Transit · Variable Stability
Count: 1–2 per galaxy depending on size. Fixed per seed.
Function: Instant transit between two fixed points. Bypasses all intermediate systems and lanes.
Stability: Wormholes destabilize based on FLEET MASS transported. Larger fleets cause more structural damage per transit. No RNG collapse rolls.
Warp Shear: Every transit deals guaranteed percentage-based hull damage (PH 10–20%, scaled to fleet tonnage) to all ships in the fleet. No coin flip — the cost is known before you commit.
Fractured State: After repeated heavy use, the wormhole permanently downgrades to Fractured. Fractured wormholes remain usable but inflict SEVERE damage (PH 30–50% hull) on any transit. One-way downgrade — cannot be repaired.
Burnout: After enough Fractured transits, the wormhole collapses permanently. Total lifetime tonnage is the deciding factor, not luck.
Strategic Cost: Turns wormholes from a slot machine into a desperate, costly tactical decision. Players can PLAN around the damage cost instead of gambling on collapse odds. Send a small scout fleet cheaply or ram your entire armada through and pay the price in hull integrity.
Discovery: Wormhole locations fixed per seed but must be discovered through exploration. Not visible on the map until a fleet or scout reaches the system.
Strategic Value: Force projection across the galaxy. Losing access to a wormhole (collapse or enemy control) can be campaign-defining.
FOG OF WAR & EXPLORATION
Limited Vision · Scout-Driven Discovery
Starting Vision: Player sees only their homeworld system and immediately adjacent systems at campaign start.
Scan Range: PH Each controlled system reveals adjacent systems. Exact range pending.
Scout Ships: PH Dedicated scout units can be sent to explore unknown systems. Faster than full fleets, minimal combat capability.
Intel Layers: System discovered (basic info: star type, planet count) vs system explored (full info: planet types, resources, garrison, traits).
Enemy Movement: Enemy fleets only visible within your scan range. Beyond that, you’re blind. Espionage operations (Ch. 24) can extend vision.
Wormhole & Relic Discovery: Hidden features (wormholes, Aethyn relics, anomalies) only revealed when a fleet physically enters the system.
Strategic Implication: Early game is exploration-driven. Mid game shifts to intelligence warfare. Late game is about maintaining vision across a sprawling empire.
Scanning: A three-tier scanning system (Approach → Orbital → Deep) reveals planetary details progressively. See Ch. 13 — Scanning & Discovery ›
