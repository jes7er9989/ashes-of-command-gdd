GROUND COMBAT
Territory Battle Model · Cover · Deployment · Reserves — Chapter 19
Inspired by Dawn of War: Dark Crusade. Planetary Map → Territory Selection → Battle Map. Each territory is a PLACE with unique terrain. Procedural battle maps from biome-specific templates. Capital territories, resource nodes, and spaceports get unique hand-crafted elements.
Ground combat terrain is determined by planet type. Each of the 17 planet types creates distinct battlefield conditions — from the open killing fields of Dead Worlds to the corridor warfare of Orbital Stations. See Ch. 13 for detailed terrain profiles per planet type.
Ground Battle Flow
Territory Selection → Deployment → Auto-Battle → Resolution
Ground combat is territory-by-territory. Each planet is divided into territories (see Ch. 13); conquering a planet means taking territories until the defender has none left. The auto-battler handles unit behavior; the commander handles strategy. CP represents your commander’s attention — how many things one person can focus on at once (see Ch. 18 for the full CP system).
BATTLE FLOW
1. Territory Selection — Choose which territory to attack based on adjacency to your held territories. Options may be limited by your space battle performance (see Ch. 18). Multiple territories can be attacked simultaneously if you have enough generals (see Ch. 22).
2. Pre-Battle Setup — Assign a general to lead the assault. Set army-level formation (determines how squads auto-arrange). Configure Rules of Engagement. Review terrain intel from scanning. Assign forces from your available pool.
3. Deployment — Forces deploy at designated drop zones. Space superiority determines drop zone quality (see Ch. 18). Reserves staged behind the front line for triggered deployment.
4. Auto-Battle — Squads execute based on formation, ROE, and terrain. The general’s personality trait colors unit behavior (see Ch. 22). Commander intervenes with CP abilities, broad orders, and targeted squad redirects (squad redirect costs CP).
5. Resolution — Battle ends when victory conditions are met (see Variable Win Conditions below). Territory control changes. Forces resupply during the mandatory pause before the next engagement. After-action report with full replay available.
THE FORCED PAUSE
Between territory battles, there is a mandatory resupply pause. Your army regroups, heals (if supply lines are intact), and replenishes. During this pause, other factions and AI make their moves. This is the breathing room that makes the game turn-based at the strategic layer and real-time at the tactical layer. Units that are supply-blocked do NOT heal or resupply between battles.
Generals in Ground Combat
Command · Death · Morale Cascade
Generals aren’t abstract. They’re on the battlefield with their troops, and they can die. A general’s personality trait affects how their entire army fights (see Ch. 22). Losing a general mid-battle is catastrophic.
GENERAL ON THE FIELD
• General is attached to a command unit on the battlefield
• Their personality trait passively modifies all units under their command (see Ch. 22 trait bonuses)
• Their CP ability is available once per battle (see Ch. 22 trait→ability mapping)
• General gains XP from battles won, units killed, objectives completed
• Promotion path: Lieutenant → Captain → Colonel → General → Commander (see Ch. 22)
GENERAL DEATH & WOUNDING
• If the general’s command unit is destroyed, the general can die
• Survival chance based on rank + bodyguard quality + terrain: PH 30-70%
• Death: Permanent loss. All units under command suffer immediate morale shock. Army-wide PH -30% combat effectiveness for remainder of battle. Standing orders default to “hold position.”
• Wounded (survived): General extracted. Out of action for PH 3-5 turns. Army takes PH -15% morale penalty. Second-in-command assumes control with reduced bonuses.
Ground Rules of Engagement
Targeting · Aggression · Formation · Retreat — Your Standing Orders
Rules of Engagement work the same way on the ground as in space (see Ch. 18) but with ground-specific options. Set them before battle or spend CP to modify mid-fight. You can go fully autonomous or micromanage every squad’s behavior.
TARGETING PRIORITY
Default: Role-based (anti-armor targets vehicles, snipers target high-value). Player can set custom priority per squad type: “Focus infantry first,” “Ignore fortifications,” “Target the general.”
AGGRESSION
Assault — Push forward aggressively. Take ground. Accept higher casualties.
Balanced — Advance with cover-to-cover movement. Standard.
Defensive — Hold position. Dig in. Only fire when engaged.
General personality modifies interpretation (Aggressive general makes “balanced” more forward).
COVER USAGE
Aggressive cover — Use cover on advance but don’t stop to entrench
Standard — Move cover-to-cover, pause to establish position
Hunker — Find best available cover and stay. Maximum defense, zero advance.
SQUAD FORMATIONS
Army-level formation set by general (see Q27). Squads auto-adopt:
Defensive line → Squads spread, dig in, overlapping fields of fire
Aggressive push → Wedge formations, lead with heavy armor
Flanking maneuver → Split force, main body pins while flankers envelope
Fighting withdrawal → Leapfrog retreat, rear guard covers
RETREAT CONDITIONS
Never — Fight to the last. Desperate situations or Reckless generals.
At threshold — Squad retreats when below PH 30% strength
On order — Only when commander spends CP to order retreat
Smart retreat — Units fall back to better cover when taking heavy fire, re-engage when able
VEHICLE RULES
Spearhead — Vehicles lead, infantry follows in cover
Support — Vehicles hang back, provide fire support for advancing infantry
Independent — Vehicles pursue own targets based on role
Vehicle type determines terrain accessibility (see Ch. 13 planet types)
Variable Win Conditions
Territory Control · Timed Hold · Defense · Extraction — Context Determines Victory
TERRITORY CAPTURE
Standard. Eliminate or rout all enemy forces from the territory. Attacker controls the territory.
TIMED HOLD
Hold a specific position (objective point) for PH X minutes. Defender must dislodge attacker before timer expires.
DEFENSE
Survive an assault for PH X waves/minutes. Reinforcements incoming. Hold until relief arrives.
EXTRACTION
Protect a specific unit or asset while it evacuates. Win condition: asset reaches extraction point. Lose condition: asset destroyed.
HAZARD DEFENSE
Defend against planetary hazards — wildlife incursion, Vorax tendril ground assault, environmental catastrophe. Both armies may fight the hazard simultaneously (temporary ceasefire or three-way battle).
SIEGE
Assault a fortified position (bunker complex, planetary fortress, shield generator). Requires sustained bombardment + infantry push. Fortifications must be destroyed before objective can be captured.
Win conditions are communicated in the Pre-Battle Window. The auto-battle AI adapts unit behavior to the objective — in a Timed Hold, units will prioritize positioning on the objective rather than chasing kills.

Deployment & Reserves
Space Performance → Beachhead Size → Reserve Triggers
ATTACKER DEPLOYMENT
Decisive Victory: 3-4 territories wide beachhead. Standard (narrow): 2 territories. Pyrrhic (loss): 1 territory cramped. No space battle: 2 standard.
RESERVE TRIGGERS
Condition A: 25% casualties → reserves deploy at rear. Condition B: forward position captured → reserves deploy at captured point. Commit reserves early (more forces now) or hold (insurance later).
