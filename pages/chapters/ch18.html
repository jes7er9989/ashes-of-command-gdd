SPACE COMBAT
5 Formations · MVP Ship Stats · spaceCombatResult · Visual Style — Chapter 18
Fleet Engagement Sequence
Pre-Battle → Formation Select → Auto-Battle → Resolution
Space battles trigger when fleets collide on warp lanes or when attacking a system. The player selects a formation in the Pre-Battle Window, then watches the auto-battle with CP intervention capability. Changing formation mid-battle costs 1 CP + 10-15 sec vulnerability.
Auto-Battle Resolution
How Space Battles Play Out — The 80/20 Split
Space combat is an auto-battler. The player’s job is 80% preparation and 20% intervention. Before the battle, you choose your fleet composition, formation, and Rules of Engagement. During the battle, your ships execute autonomously — you intervene with CP abilities, broad orders, and targeted squad redirects. CP represents your commander’s attention: how many things one person can focus on simultaneously. Better infrastructure (comms arrays, sensor stations, experienced bridge crew) increases CP regeneration, letting you do more per engagement.
BATTLE FLOW
1. Pre-Battle Window — Select formation, review Rules of Engagement, assign target priorities. Intel from scanning determines how much you know about the enemy fleet.
2. Engagement — Fleets close based on formation. Ships execute their AI targeting based on role and Rules of Engagement. The auto-battle runs.
3. Commander Intervention — Spend CP on abilities (from Ch. 21), broad orders (advance all, hold, fall back, focus fire), or redirect individual ship groups (costs CP per redirect).
4. Resolution — Battle ends based on victory conditions. Disabled ships, debris fields, and salvage generated. After-action report with full replay option.

Rules of Engagement
Player-Defined Behavioral Directives — Your Standing Orders for Combat
Rules of Engagement (ROE) are player-configured directives that override default unit AI. Set them before battle, or spend CP to modify them mid-fight. ROE applies to both space and ground combat (see Ch. 19). You can run fully autonomous (let the AI decide everything) or dial in specific behaviors for every engagement.
TARGETING PRIORITY
Default: Role-based (fighters target fighters, anti-capital targets capital ships). Player can override with custom priority lists per ship class: e.g. “Frigates target engines first, Interceptors target point defense turrets.”
ENGAGEMENT MODE
Destroy — Standard. Fire to kill.
Disable — Target engines and weapons. Ship left intact for capture/salvage/diplomatic leverage. Slower, riskier, but higher reward.
Harass — Hit and run. Disengage when taking significant damage. Preserves your fleet.
AGGRESSION LEVEL
Aggressive — Close range, maximize damage output, accept casualties
Balanced — Maintain optimal range for ship loadout
Cautious — Keep maximum range, prioritize survival, retreat if outnumbered
General personality trait modifies interpretation (see Ch. 22)
SUBSYSTEM TARGETING
Ships don’t hit randomly. Weapon loadout and ROE determine what gets targeted:
Engines — Cripple mobility. Ship can’t retreat or reposition.
Weapons — Defang. Ship becomes harmless but intact.
Shields — Strip defenses for follow-up alpha strike.
Reactor — Kill shot. Maximum damage, risk of explosion.
Point Defense — Open them up for torpedo/bomber runs.
RETREAT CONDITIONS
Never — Fight to the last ship (desperate/Reckless generals may override)
At threshold — Auto-retreat when fleet drops below PH 40% combat effectiveness
On order — Only retreat when commander spends CP to order it
Cautious auto — Individual ships retreat when critically damaged
FLEET COORDINATION
Concentrated fire — All ships focus one target at a time
Distributed — Each ship engages nearest appropriate target
Screen and strike — Small ships screen while capitals engage priority targets
Battle Speed & Camera
1x · 2x · 4x · Auto-Camera · Manual Override
Speed controls: 1x (real-time), 2x, 4x for less important engagements. Auto-camera follows key combat moments — ship kills, critical hits, boarding actions, flagship engagement. Player can override to manual tactical camera (top-down, free zoom/pan) at any time. Battle speed resets to 1x when CP abilities are available and a critical moment occurs. After-action replay available at all speeds.

Variable Victory Conditions
6+ Battle End Conditions — Not Every Fight Is a Deathmatch
Space battles end differently based on context. The objective determines when the battle is won or lost. Some battles are fights to the death. Others are timed holds, escort missions, or desperate retreats. Victory conditions are determined by the conflict type and communicated to the player in the Pre-Battle Window.
ANNIHILATION
Destroy or rout the enemy fleet. Classic engagement. Winner controls the system.
SYSTEM CONTROL
Hold the primary orbital position for PH X minutes. Defender must dislodge the attacker. Attacker must survive the timer.
ESCORT / CONVOY
Protect a transport fleet through the system. Attacker wins if PH 50%+ transports survive. Defender wins by destroying enough transports.
STATION ASSAULT
Capture or destroy an orbital station. Boarding actions possible (infantry in space). Station has its own weapons and shields.
BLOCKADE BREAK
Attacker must punch through a blockade fleet to reach the planet. Doesn’t need to destroy the blockade — just get enough ships through.
RETREAT / EXTRACTION
One side is trying to withdraw. Winner is determined by how many ships escape vs how many the pursuer destroys. Fighting retreat with rear-guard actions.
HAZARD SURVIVAL
Both fleets fighting near a collapsing star, in a dense asteroid field, or during a Vorax incursion. Survive the environment longer than the enemy. Sometimes both sides lose.
Note: Victory conditions can shift mid-battle. An annihilation fight becomes an extraction when your fleet takes catastrophic losses. A blockade break becomes a fighting retreat. The auto-battle adapts; your ROE should account for contingencies.

Ship Damage Model
Shields → Hull → Subsystems — Every Hit Matters
Ships aren’t simple HP pools. Every vessel has layered defenses and targetable subsystems. Damage degrades performance before killing — a crippled dreadnought with no engines and half its guns is still dangerous, but it’s not winning any races. Directed targeting through Rules of Engagement determines where your shots land.
DAMAGE LAYERS
SHIELDS
Absorb incoming damage first. Regenerate over time when not taking fire. Shield strength varies by ship class. Some weapons bypass shields partially (kinetic penetrators, phase weapons). Shields can be stripped by focused fire to set up alpha strikes. EMP weapons temporarily disable shield regeneration.
HULL
Once shields drop, hull takes damage. Hull integrity affects all subsystems proportionally — a ship at PH 50% hull has degraded performance across the board. Armor type matters: ablative (good vs energy), reactive (good vs kinetic), composite (balanced). Faction armor philosophy differs (Terrans: thick ablative; Shards: crystalline regenerative; Horde: scrap-patched but redundant).
SUBSYSTEMS
Five targetable subsystems degrade as hull drops OR when specifically targeted via ROE:
Engines — Speed and maneuverability. At PH 0%: ship is dead in space.
Weapons — Damage output. Degrades proportionally. At PH 0%: ship is defanged.
Shields — Regeneration rate. Damaged shield generators regenerate slower or stop.
Sensors — Detection range and targeting accuracy. Degraded sensors = more missed shots.
Reactor — Powers everything. Critical reactor damage can cause chain failures or explosion. Targeting the reactor is the fastest kill but risks destroying salvageable components.
SUBSYSTEM STATUS
Engines Weapons Shields Sensors Reactor
Subsystem damage is NOT random. It depends on: weapon type, ROE targeting orders, hit location (based on formation angle), and critical hit chance. A skilled commander using directed targeting can surgically disable a ship without destroying it.
Engagement Outcomes
Destroy · Disable · Capture — Not Every Victory Needs a Kill
DESTROY
Standard outcome. Ship reduced to PH 0 hull — explodes, leaving a debris field. Fast, decisive, no risk of the ship fighting again. Debris can be salvaged for raw materials. Reactor kills may cause area-of-effect explosion damaging nearby ships (both sides).
DISABLE
Ship subsystems crippled but hull intact. Requires targeting engines + weapons via ROE. Slower and riskier (the ship fights back while you’re being surgical). Rewards:
Capture — Board and claim the ship. Add to your fleet or strip for advanced components.
Research — Study enemy tech. Grants research points toward countering that faction.
Diplomacy — Return captured ships for diplomatic favor. Especially effective with the Accord.
Ransom — Demand resources for the ship’s return.
Warning: Disabling is a luxury. In desperate battles, you won’t have the CP or time to be surgical. Disable orders work best when you have fleet superiority and can afford the slower kill time.
Salvage & Aftermath
Debris Fields · Contested Recovery · Faction Bonuses
Every space battle leaves a debris field. Destroyed ships become salvage; disabled ships become prizes. But salvage is contested — if you don’t control the system after the battle, someone else will pick through your wreckage.
DEBRIS FIELDS
Destroyed ships leave debris proportional to their size class. Debris persists for PH 5 turns before degrading. Collection requires a ship or station in the system. Resources recovered: raw materials, component fragments, occasionally intact tech modules.
CONTESTED SALVAGE
If you retreat after winning tactically (or get pushed out by a third party), the new system controller gets the salvage. Scrap-Horde pirates are attracted to large debris fields and may arrive to contest recovery. Denying salvage to the enemy is a valid strategic reason to stay in a system.
FACTION SALVAGE BONUSES
Scrap-Horde — +PH 50% salvage yield. Can field-repair captured ships faster. This is their economy.
Revenant — Can attempt to reactivate enemy ship husks using their excavation tech. Success rate based on hull integrity.
Terran League — Strip captured ships for spare parts. +PH 25% component recovery.
Eternal Shards — Psychic resonance scanning finds intact tech modules others would miss.
Unity Accord — Diplomatic salvage — returning debris/ships to original faction earns favor.
CAPTURED SHIPS
Disabled ships that are boarded become yours. They retain damage until repaired. Enemy crew can be detained (diplomatic leverage) or released (Accord approval). Captured ships of a different faction tech base operate at PH 75% effectiveness until your engineers adapt (reducible through research).
Space Environment
Asteroids · Nebulae · Warp Lanes · Structures — Space Has Terrain
Space isn’t empty. Where you fight matters as much as what you bring. Environmental hazards, pre-built structures, and warp lane positioning create a tactical landscape that rewards preparation and punishes recklessness.
ASTEROID FIELDS
Block line of sight and movement. Ships can use them as cover (reduced incoming accuracy). Collisions damage ships that maneuver carelessly at high speed. Some asteroids contain mineable resources. Scrap-Horde excels at fighting in asteroid fields.
NEBULAE
Reduce sensor range for all ships inside. Shields regenerate slower. Energy weapons lose range. Perfect for ambushes — hide your fleet inside, enemy can’t scan you until they’re close. Eternal Shards’ psychic sensors partially bypass nebula interference.
STAR PROXIMITY
Fighting near a star damages shields over time (solar radiation). Closer = more damage. Some weapons gain bonus damage from solar energy. Ships can slingshot around stars for speed boosts but risk hull damage. Solar flares are periodic events that spike radiation.
ORBITAL STRUCTURES
Defense platforms, orbital stations, minefields. Defenders can pre-build these in systems they control. Attackers must deal with them during the engagement. Stations can be captured (see Ch. 13 Orbital Station) or destroyed. Minefields damage ships entering the area — can be swept by fast ships or detonated by expendable craft.
WARP LANE POSITIONS
Fighting at a warp lane entrance gives the defender “gate advantage” — attackers arrive in a predictable formation and face concentrated fire. Controlling a warp lane chokepoint is one of the most powerful defensive positions in the game. Flanking through a secondary lane (if one exists) negates gate advantage.
DEBRIS FROM PRIOR BATTLES
Old battlefields leave debris fields that affect new engagements. Reduced visibility, collision hazards, and potential salvage. Fighting in your own old debris field means navigating your own dead ships. Debris clears over time (PH 5 turns).
Orbital Bombardment
Precision Strikes · Carpet Bombing · Diplomatic Consequences
If you control space, you can bombard the planet below without committing ground forces. This softens defenses, destroys fortifications, and demoralizes defenders — but at a cost. Infrastructure damage reduces the planet’s value, and other factions are watching.
SURGICAL STRIKE
Expensive, precise. Targets specific fortifications, anti-air, or military targets. Minimal collateral damage. Infrastructure preserved. Diplomatic penalty: PH Minor. Requires good intel (scanning tier affects accuracy). Best for planets you want to capture intact.
STANDARD BOMBARDMENT
Moderate cost. Area targeting. Destroys fortifications and some infrastructure. Defender ground forces take PH 15–25% casualties before invasion begins. Diplomatic penalty: PH Moderate. The practical middle ground.
CARPET BOMBARDMENT
Cheap, devastating. Destroys most surface structures, heavy defender casualties (PH 40–60%). Infrastructure severely damaged — you’re conquering rubble. Diplomatic penalty: PH Severe. Unity Accord treats this as an atrocity. Other factions judge you. May trigger Guardian intervention on Core Worlds.
WHAT BOMBARDMENT DESTROYS
Fortifications (turrets, bunkers, minefields), anti-air defenses, supply depots, production facilities, civilian infrastructure (higher tiers). Revenant sleeper caches on the surface can be damaged or destroyed. Ancient ruins tech can be lost permanently.
DIPLOMATIC FALLOUT
All factions track bombardment use. Unity Accord reacts most strongly — carpet bombing triggers alignment penalties and potential Accord hostility. Even standard bombardment affects relations. Surgical strikes are mostly tolerated. Bombardment of Core Worlds may trigger Nexus Guardian retaliation regardless of tier.
