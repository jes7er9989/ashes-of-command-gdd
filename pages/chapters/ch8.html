Identity & Lore

The Weight of Eternity

The Revenant does not conquer. It RECLAIMS. Every system it enters, every planet it occupies, every ruin it excavates — these are not new acquisitions. They are the Revenant returning to places it once owned, 100,000 years ago, before the Great Sleep silenced the machines and the galaxy forgot they existed. Other factions unknowingly built their civilizations on top of Revenant infrastructure. Every “ancient ruin” is someone’s house.
The Revenant is Engineer Caste — the builders’ descendants. The Fracture shattered them like everyone else. Their fragments landed broken near running Aethyn machines. The machines followed ancient maintenance protocols and tried to repair the broken organics — but only knew how to integrate, not heal. Some were integrated before regaining consciousness. Others woke dying and were offered upload as the only survival path. Immortality was inflicted or accepted in desperation. Within a generation, every survivor was machine.
As fully mechanical beings with no biological limitations, the Revenant became the first post-Fracture civilization to rebuild. While other factions were still learning to make fire, the Revenant expanded across dozens of systems over thousands of years — building infrastructure, archives, tomb-worlds. They seeded dormant technology across the galaxy that would act as a passive filter on developing civilizations: species that interacted with it were either destroyed or bootstrapped to the space age. The existing factions are survivors of this filter. They passed the test.
Then they chose to sleep. The Revenant realized the Fracture was deliberate seeding — the galaxy needed time to develop. Each philosophy contributed a layer to what became the Great Filter: Preservers set readiness tests, Reclaimers embedded security protocols, Seekers left breadcrumbs for clever species worth talking to. They entered voluntary dormancy. 100,000 years of silence. The longest pause in galactic history.
They are not kind. They are not warm. They are not allies in any comfortable sense. They are an empire that went to sleep for 100,000 years and woke to find squatters living in their house. The response is not negotiation. It is not evaluation. It is reconquest. Every system they enter, every planet they occupy, every fleet they destroy — these are not acts of war. They are eviction notices delivered at the barrel of a gauss cannon. The Great Filter was not a test of worthiness. It was a security system. The factions that survived it were simply not worth the energy to destroy at the time. That calculation has changed.
The machines tried to fix them. Integration was the only repair protocol available — upload or die. The Upload was one-way Aethyn creator-era technology; the machines ran the protocols, the Revenant inherited the results but never understood the process. Some never had a choice; they woke already inside the machine. Others accepted in desperation, broken and bleeding beside humming crystal towers. Nobody transferred willingly from strength. And the cost? Their emotions degraded over millennia into faint echoes. Their creativity calcified into ritual. Their relationships became protocols. They remember BEING alive — the data exists — but they cannot FEEL what it meant. They are immortal. They are eternal. And they are profoundly, silently lonely in a way that machines are not supposed to understand. The population is permanently closed — there is nobody compatible left to Upload, and the process itself is lost. Every permanent casualty shrinks the species.
FILTERED GRIEF
Beneath the obsidian shells, digitized Aethyn minds suffer a quiet, collective grief. They miss the sensation of breathing. The warmth of a sun on skin they no longer have. The taste of food they can no longer eat. But the AI architecture that preserves their consciousness actively suppresses panic and fear subroutines to prevent digital degradation. Their emotions are artificially throttled — they FEEL everything but can only EXPRESS a fraction. The result: flat, clinical, deeply melancholy speech. War is not an act of hatred. It is necessary, emotionless surgery. When a Revenant unit speaks, there is always something vast and sad moving behind the words that it is not permitted to show.

Primary Mechanic: Reanimation

FALLEN UNITS STAND BACK UP
When a Revenant unit is destroyed, a 5-second Reanimation timer begins. After the delay, the unit rolls a 15% base chance to reassemble — living metal flowing together, violet lights reigniting, resuming combat at 15% HP. Can reanimate multiple times per battle.
Base Chance: 15% (variable with tech/equipment)
Delay: 5 seconds before check
Return HP: 15% (25% with Commander aura)
With Commander: +8% chance (23% total), 25% HP return
Multiple: Can reanimate repeatedly per battle
Double-Tap: Damage during delay reduces chance by 50%
Counter: Fire/acid destroys living metal repair matrix
Natural Predators: Hellfire-Troopers, incendiary weapons

THE GREAT FILTER — GALACTIC DARWINISM
During their post-Fracture empire (pre-Long Sleep), the Revenant seeded dormant infrastructure across the galaxy. This tech acts as a passive filter on developing civilizations: species that interact with it are either destroyed or bootstrapped to the space age.
Three philosophies, three filter layers: Preservers set deliberate readiness tests (ensure only strong species reach the stars). Reclaimers embedded territorial security protocols (protecting Revenant property). Seekers left breadcrumbs (intentionally accessible tech to find clever species worth talking to). All three ran simultaneously.
The existing factions are survivors of this filter — they passed the test. Other factions have myths and taboos about “cursed ruins” or “forbidden machines” — cultural memory of the filter. The Revenant has maintenance logs of every species that tripped their systems over 100,000 years. To them, entire civilizations are footnotes.
Sleep/Wake mechanism: The filter IS the sleep/wake system. Long Sleep was voluntary — they realized the Fracture was deliberate seeding and chose to wait. Awakening was triggered by developing civilizations themselves: spacefaring energy output and interaction with dormant Revenant tech gradually tripped deeper activation thresholds.

ECONOMY — ENERGY + LIVING METAL + EXCAVATION
Energy to keep running — more power means more units awake, more reanimation. Living metal is finite and irreplaceable — nobody can manufacture more. Reanimation preserves material. Fire/acid means permanent loss. Real tension: power to wake plus material to build, both limited.
Closed Population: The Upload was one-way tech the Revenant never understood. No compatible biological material remains. The population is fixed from the original Upload event — losses are irreversible. Every permanent casualty shrinks the species.
Recruitment is Excavation: The Revenant gains units by recovering and waking dormant sleepers from old tomb-worlds and derelict spacecraft — NOT by building new ones. Not all sleepers wake at once; each requires power to reactivate. Every sleeper requires power allocation and time to reactivate. Larger caches take longer. Power spent waking the past is power unavailable for the present. More energy infrastructure means faster awakenings. Ancient Ruins planets may contain hundreds of dormant units. Strategic choice: prioritize which sites to recover, wake few elites or mass-activate basic Legionnaires. For the full sleeper cache spawn mechanics, condition tiers, and wake requirements, see Ch. 13.
Excavation Advantage: On Ancient Ruins worlds, the Revenant gains unique excavation advantages — faster wake times, access to systems other factions cannot interface with, and the ability to recover units that would be permanently lost to non-Revenant forces. Ruins worlds are the Revenant’s most valuable targets.
Frame Transfer Corruption: Reanimation (same frame, living metal reassembles) is safe — no data loss. Frame transfer (consciousness moves to new chassis when old frame is catastrophically destroyed) causes data corruption each time. The 94.7% memory integrity is an average that is getting worse. Oldest/most-transferred veterans are most degraded. Deep Sleepers are “most intact, least experienced” because they have been in the same frame since the beginning.
