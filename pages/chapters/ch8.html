Identity & Lore
The Weight of Eternity
The Necro-Legion does not conquer. It RECLAIMS. Every system it enters, every planet it occupies, every ruin it excavates — these are not new acquisitions. They are the Necro-Legion returning to places it once owned, 100,000 years ago, before the Great Sleep silenced the machines and the galaxy forgot they existed. Other factions unknowingly built their civilizations on top of Necro-Legion infrastructure. Every “ancient ruin” is someone’s house.
The Necro-Legion is Engineer Caste — the builders’ descendants. The Fracture shattered them like everyone else. Their fragments landed broken near running Aethyn machines. The machines followed ancient maintenance protocols and tried to repair the broken organics — but only knew how to integrate, not heal. Some were integrated before regaining consciousness. Others woke dying and were offered upload as the only survival path. Immortality was inflicted or accepted in desperation. Within a generation, every survivor was machine.
As fully mechanical beings with no biological limitations, the Necro-Legion became the first post-Fracture civilization to rebuild. While other factions were still learning to make fire, the Legion expanded across dozens of systems over thousands of years — building infrastructure, archives, tomb-worlds. They seeded dormant technology across the galaxy that would act as a passive filter on developing civilizations: species that interacted with it were either destroyed or bootstrapped to the space age. The existing factions are survivors of this filter. They passed the test.
Then they chose to sleep. The Legion realized the Fracture was deliberate seeding — the galaxy needed time to develop. Each philosophy contributed a layer to what became the Great Filter: Preservers set readiness tests, Reclaimers embedded security protocols, Seekers left breadcrumbs for clever species worth talking to. They entered voluntary dormancy. 100,000 years of silence. The longest pause in galactic history.
They are not kind. They are not warm. They are not allies in any comfortable sense. They are cold, methodical, patient, and utterly relentless machines driven by a single imperative: preserve the record. Destroy the entropy. Ensure that what WAS continues to BE, regardless of whether anyone living cares.
The machines tried to fix them. Integration was the only repair protocol available — upload or die. The Upload was one-way Aethyn creator-era technology; the machines ran the protocols, the Legion inherited the results but never understood the process. Some never had a choice; they woke already inside the machine. Others accepted in desperation, broken and bleeding beside humming crystal towers. Nobody transferred willingly from strength. And the cost? Their emotions degraded over millennia into faint echoes. Their creativity calcified into ritual. Their relationships became protocols. They remember BEING alive — the data exists — but they cannot FEEL what it meant. They are immortal. They are eternal. And they are profoundly, silently lonely in a way that machines are not supposed to understand. The population is permanently closed — there is nobody compatible left to Upload, and the process itself is lost. Every permanent casualty shrinks the species.

Primary Mechanic: Reanimation
FALLEN UNITS STAND BACK UP
When a Necro-Legion unit is destroyed, a 5-second Reanimation timer begins. After the delay, the unit rolls a 15% base chance to reassemble — living metal flowing together, green lights reigniting, resuming combat at 15% HP. Can reanimate multiple times per battle.
Base Chance: 15% (variable with tech/equipment)
Delay: 5 seconds before check
Return HP: 15% (25% with Commander aura)
With Commander: +8% chance (23% total), 25% HP return
Multiple: Can reanimate repeatedly per battle
Double-Tap: Damage during delay reduces chance by 50%
Counter: Fire/acid destroys living metal repair matrix

THE GREAT FILTER — GALACTIC DARWINISM
During their post-Fracture empire (pre-Long Sleep), the Necro-Legion seeded dormant infrastructure across the galaxy. This tech acts as a passive filter on developing civilizations: species that interact with it are either destroyed or bootstrapped to the space age.
Three philosophies, three filter layers: Preservers set deliberate readiness tests (ensure only strong species reach the stars). Reclaimers embedded territorial security protocols (protecting Necro-Legion property). Seekers left breadcrumbs (intentionally accessible tech to find clever species worth talking to). All three ran simultaneously.
The existing factions are survivors of this filter — they passed the test. Other factions have myths and taboos about “cursed ruins” or “forbidden machines” — cultural memory of the filter. The Necro-Legion has maintenance logs of every species that tripped their systems over 100,000 years. To them, entire civilizations are footnotes.
Sleep/Wake mechanism: The filter IS the sleep/wake system. Long Sleep was voluntary — they realized the Fracture was deliberate seeding and chose to wait. Awakening was triggered by developing civilizations themselves: spacefaring energy output and interaction with dormant Necro tech gradually tripped deeper activation thresholds.

ECONOMY — ENERGY + LIVING METAL + EXCAVATION
Energy to keep running — more power means more units awake, more reanimation. Living metal is finite and irreplaceable — nobody can manufacture more. Reanimation preserves material. Fire/acid means permanent loss. Real tension: power to wake plus material to build, both limited.
Closed Population: The Upload was one-way tech the Legion never understood. No compatible biological material remains. The population is fixed from the original Upload event — losses are irreversible. Every permanent casualty shrinks the species.
Recruitment is Excavation: The Necro-Legion gains units by recovering and waking dormant sleepers from old tomb-worlds and derelict spacecraft — NOT by building new ones. Not all sleepers wake at once; each requires power to reactivate. More energy infrastructure means faster awakenings. Ancient Ruins planets may contain hundreds of dormant units. Strategic choice: prioritize which sites to recover, wake few elites or mass-activate basic Legionnaires.
Frame Transfer Corruption: Reanimation (same frame, living metal reassembles) is safe — no data loss. Frame transfer (consciousness moves to new chassis when old frame is catastrophically destroyed) causes data corruption each time. The 94.7% memory integrity is an average that is getting worse. Oldest/most-transferred veterans are most degraded. Deep Sleepers are “most intact, least experienced” because they have been in the same frame since the beginning.
