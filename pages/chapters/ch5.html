Descended from ALL Aethyn castes — the most complete echo of the original species. Where other factions specialized (Shards=Scholar, Horde=Warrior, Necro=Engineer, Accord=Shepherd), the Terrans retained all four bloodlines in diluted form. Jack of all trades, master of none. Identity & Lore
Survival at Any Cost
The Terran League is humanity's last organized military force — a gritty, desperate coalition of colonial remnants, military holdouts, and civilian survivors who clung to existence after the original civilization's collapse. They are not a gleaming galactic empire. They are not an advanced post-scarcity utopia. They are soldiers, engineers, and laborers held together by stubbornness, standardized equipment, and the refusal to lie down and die.
Their strength is not elegance or technological supremacy — it is ADAPTABILITY. The Terran League fields the most versatile combined-arms military in the galaxy. Their infantry can be equipped for any role. Their vehicles are modular, repairable, and mass-produced. Their ships are ugly, over-engineered, and built to survive damage that would cripple more advanced vessels. They win not through a single overwhelming advantage but through the ability to respond to ANY situation with a competent, well-supplied, well-coordinated force.
Their weakness is that they are ordinary. They don't have psychic powers. They don't regenerate from death. They don't have railguns that fire at relativistic speeds. They have ballistic rifles, composite armor, and diesel engines. Against the galaxy's horrors — ancient machines, psychic warriors, silicon berserkers, precision zealots, adaptive biological swarms, and energy gods — the Terran League's greatest weapon is the same thing it has always been: the willingness of individual humans to fight, suffer, and die for the people standing next to them.
Visual Identity
Military Industrial Realism
Ground Forces
Exposed treads on heavy vehicles. Riveted ablative armor plate — thick, overlapping slabs of composite material bolted together with visible fasteners. Hydraulic exosuits that hiss and groan with servo strain. Belt-fed ballistic weapons that eject physical brass casings. Olive drab, gunmetal grey, burnt orange hazard markings. Everything scratched, dented, field-repaired.
Air Forces
Angular, swept-wing aircraft with visible engine intakes and physical afterburner cones. VTOL craft held aloft by massive rotary fans that kick up dust storms on landing. Bombers with exposed payload bays large enough to walk through. Terran aircraft are LOUD. Engine roar, sonic booms, the whistle of dropping munitions.
Space Fleet
Wedge-shaped capital ships layered in modular armor blocks replaceable in drydock. Massive chemical thruster arrays that glow orange during acceleration. Macro-cannon turrets the size of buildings, visibly loading physical shells on hydraulic rails. Flight decks crawling with EVA mechanics. The fleet aesthetic is naval — warships in the Age of Sail tradition.

Faction Bible — Phase 2 Lore
Terran League Faction Bible
These are procedural-generation seeds, not fixed scripts. They define the cultural DNA that the game engine uses to generate names, events, dialogue, building descriptions, and emergent narrative. 26 decisions locked in Phase 2.1 lore workshop (2026-03-13).
EMOTIONAL CORE
Defiance — “The galaxy keeps trying to kill us and we’re still here.”
Stubbornness as identity. Every setback is proof they’re tough enough to take it. Bloody-minded refusal to die. Battle scars as badges of honor. “Remember [disaster]” is a rallying cry, not a tragedy. Underdog energy even when they’re not underdogs. Dark side: Chip on the shoulder. Pick fights to prove toughness. Can’t accept help without feeling weak. Tyrant corruption: Defiance becomes aggression — “we won’t be beaten” transforms into “we’ll beat everyone first.”
GOVERNMENT
Hybrid — Civilian + Military Veto. Real elections, real civilian institutions — but the Admiralty Board can override on “security matters.” And everything is a security matter. The system WANTS to be democratic but keeps compromising. Rich internal tension between civilian and military wings that the Commander navigates.
HOMEWORLD — TERRA / “THE ROCK”
Official: Terra. Colloquial: “The Rock.” Valerius says “Terra.” Krell says “The Rock.” Vane says “Homeworld” because she doesn’t get sentimental. An Aethyn relic still sits beneath the surface — the League’s deepest secret, stabilized but never understood.
FOUNDING CRISIS — AETHYN RELIC AWAKENING
An ancient Aethyn structure buried beneath Terra powered up. Earthquakes, energy discharges, environmental chaos. Every nation on the planet pooled resources or died. They stabilized the relic without understanding it. The hidden truth: It was a Strategist fragment activating. Their entire civilization was founded on a notification bell that nobody could read. Every layer of Terran society traces back to this one Aethyn artifact they still don’t understand.
POST-FRACTURE HISTORY — CRISIS UNIFICATION
Aethyn remnants scattered on one world. Thousands of years rebuilding from near-zero. Multiple nations, competing cultures, resource wars. Then the relic beneath the surface activated — the crisis forced every nation to pool resources or perish. The Terran League was born not from ideology but from necessity. Unity through survival, not philosophy.
ORIGIN BELIEF — FRAGMENTED
Multiple competing theories about where they came from. No dominant narrative. Academics argue. Frontier folk don’t care. Fringe groups have their own theories — some surprisingly close to the truth. Nobody knows about the Aethyn. Nobody has connected the dots because nobody talks to each other.
RELIGION — SECULAR PRAGMATISM
“We don’t pray. We prepare.” No dominant religion. Trust in institutions, science, military. Fringe relic cults and frontier mysticism tolerated but not mainstream. Lore payoff: When the Strategist speaks, it shatters the secular worldview. The fringe believers are vindicated. Everything changes.
RELIC RELATIONSHIP — MIXED LAYERS
Academic: underfunded university departments studying “Precursor artifacts.” Military: Admiralty quietly collecting interesting ones. Public: most don’t care. Fringe: cults with surprisingly accurate intuitions dressed in religious language. Nobody connected the dots because nobody talks to each other. The Commander’s connection forces all three information pools together for the first time.
MILITARY DOCTRINE — THREE-TIER ADAPTIVE
Default (winning): Adaptive Pragmatism — field commanders have wide latitude. “Commander’s discretion” is doctrine.
Holding ground: Fortification & Counterattack — dig in, let them commit, hit back hard. Patient, devastating.
Losing: Attritional Resolve — take the hit, keep going, deep reserves, grind. “Acceptable losses” starts getting said too much.
Vorax problem: Attrition against a swarm that grows from what it kills is a death spiral. Greatest strength becomes greatest vulnerability.
TECH AESTHETIC — CLEAN CORE, WORN FRONTIER
Core systems: standardized, polished, professional — Mass Effect Alliance feel. Frontier: battle-scarred, patched, field-repaired, paint scratched to bare metal — Band of Brothers feel. The gradient tells you where you are in Terran space. Admiral’s flagship gleams. Frontier garrison tanks have three different paint jobs from salvage.
SOCIAL STRUCTURE — FRONTIER SPIRIT + MERITOCRATIC MELTING POT
Geographic axis: Core worlds = civilized, comfortable, institutional. Frontier = rough, independent, self-reliant. Different cultures within one League. Social axis: Competence is currency everywhere. Core meritocracy = education, innovation. Frontier meritocracy = survival, resourcefulness. Best soldiers from frontier, best officers from core, best Commanders from wherever. Secession threats every decade. League holds because alternatives are worse.
ECONOMY — MIXED MARKET
Core: Functioning markets, banking, corporate influence, trade agreements. Frontier: Barter, salvage, practical exchange. Two economies in one League. Market crashes devastate core while frontier shrugs (already self-sufficient). Resource shortages hit frontier while core debates relief packages. The economic divide mirrors everything else.
XENOPHOBIA — PRAGMATIC TOLERANCE
“Can you fight? Can you trade? Welcome aboard.” Species-second, competence-first. Official laws are fair. People are complicated. A Shards diplomat respected in chambers, stared at in the mess hall. Tolerance through pragmatism, not idealism. Don’t hate aliens, don’t have time for dead weight.
NAMING CONVENTIONS
Personal: Multicultural formal (Tanaka, Okafor, Vasquez, Petrov, Chen) + earned callsigns (“Hammer-6,” “Bulwark Actual,” “Redline”). Birth name = who you were. Callsign = who you became. Ships: TLS-074 “Defiant” (designation + earned name). Places: Official names nobody uses (“Kepler-IV Admin District” = “The Bulwark”).
INTERNAL FACTIONS ON TERRA
Mixed: warlord strongholds (conquest), ideological movements (conversion), functioning city-states (negotiation). Every territory has its own personality. HOW you unify Terra shapes the League’s future: conquer-heavy = military/frontier culture dominates. Negotiate-heavy = political/core culture grows. The core/frontier divide starts on day one.
FIRST CONTACT DOCTRINE
Player choice moment — advisors disagree, Commander decides. No scripted first contact. Who you meet depends on expansion direction. Valerius: “Open a channel. Show them we’re civilized.” Krell: “Defensive perimeter. Watch their movements.” Vane: “Say nothing. I need 48 hours to learn everything about them.”
Faction Bible — Advisors
Terran Advisors — Full Profiles
MAJOR VALERIUS — ARCHITECT ADVISOR
Background: Decorated frontline officer who chose command over combat. Realized he saved more lives from the command table than the trench. Some see the transfer as cowardice.
Core wound: Survivor's guilt. The soldiers he left behind haunt him. Chose the bigger picture but the small picture still burns.
Age/feel: Late 40s. Old scars under a pressed uniform. Speaks softly — been screaming inside for twenty years.
Hidden depth: Family in a sector he calculated as "acceptable losses." Heart disagrees. Deflects once before admitting.
Why Architect: Saw what happens when strategy fails. Swore planning would never be the weak link.
GENERAL KRELL — VANGUARD ADVISOR
Background: Frontier born, combat forged. First battle at 16 when raiders hit his colony. Rose through raw field performance — no academy, no connections.
Core wound: Can't function in peacetime. Tried leave once. Came back in 3 days. Silence was worse than shooting.
Age/feel: Early 50s. Built like a weapon. Face like a topographic map of every war. Short sentences — long ones feel like wasted ammo.
Hidden depth: Unbroken win record. Kill count behind his eyes. Late campaign: "Was any of it worth it?"
Why Vanguard: Combat is the only place the galaxy makes sense.
INQUISITOR VANE — TYRANT ADVISOR
Background: Intelligence prodigy. Recruited at 19. Pattern recognition off the charts. The line between analysis and assassination blurred one report at a time.
The break: Discovered a plot against Terran colonies. Reported through channels. Channels were too slow. Thousands died.
Core wound: Knowing isn't enough. Acting is. Even if acting is ugly. If information without action is complicity, she will never be complicit again.
Age/feel: Mid-30s. Youngest by a decade. Looks like she should be at a university. Voice like a threat assessment in progress.
Hidden depth: Was an idealist. Brilliant, naive, full of hope. One crisis broke her. Not evil — TERRIFIED. The most dangerous person on the command deck because she is not wrong about the galaxy, only about herself.
Why Tyrant: Information is power. She uses it the way the galaxy demands — ruthlessly.
ADVISOR CHEMISTRY: CROSSED PATHS
They know OF each other. Never worked together before the Commander assignment. Valerius read Vane's briefs — respects the mind, distrusts the methods. Vane analyzed Krell's campaigns — admires results, sees human cost as data. Krell felt Valerius's reforms — "some desk officer reshuffled my supply lines." Trust is built in-game, not pre-loaded.

VORAX ADAPTATION — VERSATILE GENERALIST
Mixed-caste DNA produces jack-of-all-trades Vorax. Not strongest in any category but competent in ALL. The “normal” faction produces the most dangerous adaptation because the swarm becomes versatile with no exploitable weakness. No gimmick. No counter. Just competence at everything.
NEGOTIATION AT THE NEXUS
Military Respect + Defiant Honesty + Pragmatic Edge. Soldier to soldier: “You held the line.” Core honesty: “We don’t know what we are. We came anyway.” Pragmatic edge: “The galaxy is burning. Let’s figure this out.” Warden-Commander recognizes: mixed-caste descendant embodying what the Aethyn lost.
CASCADE VULNERABILITY — 3 PRESSURE POINTS
Supply Lines: Cut → frontier starves → secession threats → League fractures.
Political Fracture: Crisis → civilian/military veto standoff → governmental paralysis.
Morale Collapse: Losses → attrition fatigue → Defiance spirit cracking → breaking point.
DIVERGENCE — THREE INDEPENDENT AXES
Government: Democratic ↔ Authoritarian
Culture: Core ↔ Frontier dominant
Doctrine: Shaped by enemies fought (anti-Horde = aggressive, anti-Shards = paranoid, anti-Necro = tech-embrace, anti-Vorax = fortress attrition)
