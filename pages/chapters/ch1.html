GAME IDENTITY & VISION
Genre Fusion · Elevator Pitch · Core Pillars — Chapter 1
ELEVATOR PITCH
You are a weapon. A freshly decanted clone — or something stranger — created by a fallen civilization to win a war that already destroyed them once. Prove your genius across simulated campaigns. When judged ready, wake into the real galaxy — devoured from outside by an adaptive swarm, guarded at its core by ancient energy gods. Your simulated victories shaped the galaxy you now face. If you die here, it is permanent.
Genre Fusion — Four Pillars

4X · Auto-Battler · Rogue-Lite · Deep Narrative

4X GRAND STRATEGY
Macro-level galactic conquest: explore, expand, exploit, exterminate across procedural star systems. Dozens of cycles. Infrastructure, diplomacy, logistics. Emperor managing a galaxy-spanning civilization.
RTS AUTO-BATTLER
Micro-level combat on SC2-scale maps. Strategic decisions already made (composition, equipment, deployment). Player acts as general: CP abilities, reserves, observation. Units fight autonomously.
ROGUE-LITE META-PROGRESSION
Fresh galaxy per run. Death resets in-run progress. Blueprints, resources, lore persist across runs through multi-layered Archive. Veteran players always feel experience translating into power.
DEEP NARRATIVE LAYERS
You are a specific entity — clone, AI, psychic echo, construct, or reactivated core. Three named advisors with their own agendas. Procedural memories. A choice about what kind of leader you become.
THE EXPERIENCE STATEMENT
Hour 1 — Captain: Personally selecting every unit's equipment. Agonizing over deployment. Celebrating individual squad survival.
Hour 10 — Admiral: Multiple fronts. Delegating skirmishes to Commanders. Named veterans earning General rank. Choosing which front gets reinforced.
Hour 20 — Emperor: Managing autonomous Generals. Alliance politics. After-action reports from battles never witnessed. Preparing for the final push to the Core.
Emotional arc: learning → mastery → delegation → trust → consequence.
Core Pillars

Every Design Decision Must Serve At Least One

PILLAR 1: Strategic Depth Without Micromanagement
Units fight autonomously. Player decides composition, equipment, positioning. The 80% pre-battle / 20% intervention split.
PILLAR 2: Characters, Not Numbers
Units gain names, traits, rivalries, loyalty. Generals have personalities that affect autonomous behavior. Advisors are real people.
PILLAR 3: The Galaxy Is Alive
Vorax consume from the edges. Guardians wait at the Core. AI factions pursue their own agendas. Dynamic hazards reshape the map. Nothing waits for the player.
PILLAR 4: Choices Have Weight
Alignment tracks every decision. Generals remember treatment. Betrayals have consequences. The Final War galaxy is shaped by cumulative simulation choices.
Visual & Tonal References

Precise Design Targets

StarCraft II — Visual readability benchmark. Triple-redundant identification: faction color + unique silhouette + colored health bars.
Dawn of War: Dark Crusade — Territory conquest. Every territory is a PLACE, not a hex. Grimdark atmosphere. The weight of war.
Command & Conquer 3 — Diegetic UI. The interface IS a military command console. Faction-specific UI skins. Holographic projection aesthetic.
Warhammer 40K — Faction identity through silhouette. Every faction recognizable from black-and-white screenshots of unit outlines alone.
