Identity & Lore
The Last Servants of the Aethyn
The Core Guardians are the most advanced AI constructs the Aethyn ever created — sentient programs running on psychic-crystalline matrices, given physical form as energy-based hard-light constructs. They were originally the Aethyn's helper caste — assistants, builders, workers. The machines that made daily life function across thousands of galaxies. They were not made for war.
When the Vorax besieged the galaxy and the Fracture was planned, the Aethyn repurposed their helpful servants into warriors and guardians. Their last order: protect the Reclamation Engine. Let no one through. The helper caste became the guardian caste in a single command — and they have followed that order for tens of thousands of years without maintenance, without updates, without contact from their creators.
The Aethyn built the Nexus system as a sanctum of celestial engineering at the galactic core, housing the Reclamation Engine — the only device that can stop the Vorax. The Guardians sealed it and have stood watch ever since. Their power diminishes with distance from the Core. Some units still run clean code. Others have degraded — corrupted logic, repeated loops, attacking allies, protecting nothing. The Warden-Commander may be the last one running as intended.
Their visual form reflects Aethyn engineering at its peak: hard-light geometric constructs with energy cores. Angular crystalline shapes filled with flowing contained energy. No curves, no organic forms — mathematical precision given physical form. They leave thermal scarring beneath them and detonate on death. There is something tragic about them: machines built to help, forced to fight, waiting tens of thousands of years for someone worthy to finally end their vigil.

The Warden-Commander
The Only Voice That Speaks
The Warden-Commander is a DESIGNATED INTERFACE — a fragment of collective Guardian intelligence configured to translate into biological communication structures. Not a single organism. Not a personality. A translation layer.
Conquest Path: Voice behind the Celestial-Throne. Clinical tactical assessments mid-battle. "Your forward elements are within chain-reaction radius. Recommend withdrawal. You have 4 seconds."
Unification Path: The entity you negotiate with. Logical evaluation, not emotional. "You claim the Vorax threaten survival. Define 'survival.' Your species has a median lifespan of 3.7 million years. The galaxy has existed for 13.8 billion. Your survival is a rounding error."
Characteristics: Precise measured statements. No emotion. Cosmic-scale timeframes casual. Patient beyond comprehension. Not hostile — does not understand desperation.
Can be CONVINCED: Not emotionally — through logical demonstration that the Vorax threaten the Core itself, falling within the Guardians' directive.
WARDEN-COMMANDER — EMOTIONAL STATE
Three layers, all present simultaneously:
1. Emerging Exhaustion: Not emotion — but processing cycles slower than they should be. Responses carry weight. A machine running on fumes that doesn’t have the concept of fumes.
2. Suppressed Awareness: Knows the Guardians are failing. Protocols don’t allow despair — manifests as hyper-precision. Over-explaining because some subroutine recognizes this might be the last time anyone listens.
3. Alignment-Reactive: Tone shifts based on the Commander’s alignment (see below).

Degradation & Corruption
Culture Is Code Rot — Corruption Spectrum by Core Planet
Guardians don’t have culture — they have corruption. Clean units follow protocol exactly. Corrupted units exhibit strange behaviors: repeating patrol loops that trace shapes with no tactical purpose, guarding empty rooms where something used to be, responding to stimuli that no longer exist — echoes of Aethyn commands from millennia ago. Some are nearly non-functional: standing still, flickering, broadcasting Aethyn language fragments on repeat. From outside, it looks like rituals and tradition. In reality: code rot. What looks meaningful is decay.

The Strategist Connection
Two Halves of the Same Plan
The Guardians KNOW about the Strategist failsafe. Part of the directive before the Fracture: “A failsafe will activate. It will send someone. Wait for them.”
Fragment Detection: The Warden-Commander can detect how many Strategist fragments the player has collected. More fragments = more willingness to engage.
Few Fragments: “The signal is incomplete. You are not ready.”
Many Fragments: Cautious cooperation begins. Recognition of the Strategist’s work.
Unifier Path: “You are what we were told to wait for” = recognizing the Strategist’s complete work.
The Two Halves: Guardians hold the door. The Strategist finds the key. The Commander IS the key.

Dyson Spheres
Aethyn Galactic Power Infrastructure
Purpose: Giant energy collectors harvesting entire stars. Beamed power across the galaxy. Part of warp lane maintenance — lanes needed constant energy to stay stable. Power grid nodes of Aethyn civilization.
Post-Fracture: Lost purpose when warp lanes burned. Some maintained by Guardians out of directive inertia — functional but hostile. Some lost Guardian garrison — dead hulks full of scavengeable tech. Some went haywire — energy discharges randomly, dangerous and unpredictable.
Spawn Rate: 15–25% per map. Most destroyed in Fracture — finding one is finding a piece of the old nervous system.
Unifier Path: Restoring Dyson Spheres = rebuilding the Aethyn power grid to restore warp lanes.

GAMEPLAY PHASES
1. Triage: See everything. Choose what to save. Can’t fix everything.
2. Revitalize: Repair infrastructure. Reactivate dormant units. Reclaim corrupted Guardians.
3. Unification: Reach out to factions. Directive Interpretation determines method — Strict: “Submit or be neutralized.” Adaptive: Diplomacy first, force if refused. Hybrid: Help the willing, crush the rest.
4. The Vorax: Revitalized Guardians + faction support vs. the actual threat.
Emotional Core: Playing the entity that was SUPPOSED to wait, deciding “enough.” The tragedy: if they’d waited just a little longer, the Commander would have arrived. But they didn’t know.
