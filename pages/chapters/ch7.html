AETHYN ANCESTRY — WARRIOR CASTE
Descended from the Aethyn Warrior Caste — protectors, soldiers, tacticians who fought the Vorax during the Siege. The Fracture hit them mid-battle — the psychic shock fused their mineral biology into something cruder, tougher, angrier. Broken soldiers who welded themselves back together and kept fighting. Biology: MINERAL-BASED — iron and calcium lifeforms. They oxidize, corrode, rust. Consuming metal is nutrition. The forge is medicine, birthplace, and temple. Psychic attunement: MODERATE. Residual battle instincts. Can sense Aethyn relics but experience them as rage, battle-fury, and echoes of the Vorax war — “The Engine speaks.”
BORN OF IRON
The Scrap-Horde are not wearing scrap. They ARE metal. Mineral-based biology built on iron and calcium — their bones are literal iron, their blood carries metallic compounds, their skin rusts in oxygen-rich atmospheres. They eat metal. They digest ore. Their young are pulled from the ground, not born. Their Commander — The Forged Construct — is drawn from a vat of molten silicon in the First Forge. Understanding this changes everything: their scavenging is not poverty, it is feeding. Their armor is not protection, it is growth. They are Ork chaos-joy married to Dwarven iron-honor.
THE FORGED CONSTRUCT
Not a robot. Not a cyborg. Not a machine piloted by a mind. The Forged Construct is the purest, most terrifying manifestation of Horde biology — mineral life refined to its absolute limit. Where standard Horde warriors are rusted iron and corroded calcium, the Construct is drawn directly from a vat of molten silicon in the First Forge: completely uncorroded, still burning with raw thermal heat, living slag given shape and will. On the battlefield, the visual hierarchy is instant. Every other Horde warrior is weathered, oxidized, patched with scrap. The Commander is the only one still glowing — a white-hot silhouette walking through a rusted army, trailing heat shimmer and forge-light. Where the Horde is iron that has cooled and cracked, the Construct is iron that never stopped burning.
Identity & Lore

The Joy of War

The Scrap-Horde fights because fighting is GLORIOUS. They are mineral-based lifeforms — iron and calcium biology, dense as ore, stocky as foundation stones, and loud as a forge at full blast. They literally rust. They oxidize, corrode, and weather. “The Rust Tide” is not a metaphor — it’s what they ARE. And they are having the time of their lives.
These are warriors who name their guns and get genuinely upset when they break. Who argue about which explosion was better mid-battle. Who consider “it only blew up twice” a glowing product review. Who have drinking songs about famous last stands, sung in deep, gravelly, iron-resonant voices around forge-fires that never go out. They think diplomacy is “hitting them until they agree.” The comedy is real. The joy is real. The Scrap-Horde is the faction that makes you laugh AND respect them.
Their homeworld is Rusthold — a mineral-rich moon mined hollow from the inside, forge-cities filling its caverns, iron veins in the walls that are literally food. Ancient derelict warships captured and welded to the surface. An orbiting asteroid belt of smaller rocks connected by scrap-bridges and tow cables, each controlled by a different clan. Part moon, part scrapyard, part shipwreck, part fortress. Grown over centuries. Nobody planned it. Sections break off sometimes. They weld them back. It shouldn’t work. It works anyway.
The Horde originated from the Aethyn Warrior Caste — the protectors who fought the Vorax head-on during the Siege. The Fracture hit them mid-battle. The psychic shock fused their mineral biology into something cruder, tougher, angrier. Broken soldiers who welded themselves back together and kept fighting. Cut off from crystal-psychic technology, they rebuilt from scrap — turning wreckage into weapons, consuming metal for sustenance. The forge became medicine, temple, and birthplace. Working metal is working with their own biology.
Beneath the chaos runs dwarven bedrock: iron-oaths that are unbreakable. Grudge Records carved in metal and NEVER forgotten. Forge-worship where the act of forging IS prayer and master smiths are priests. Clan bonds deeper than iron veins. Betray a forge-brother and the entire clan hunts you. The Horde is 70% chaotic joy, 30% iron honor — and the 30% is the part that will kill you.
The Scrap-Horde does not innovate. They IMPROVISE. Their technology is stolen, salvaged, reverse-engineered, and bolted together with welding torches and profanity. A Scrap-Horde tank might have a Terran League chassis, an Eternal Shards energy coil wired backward into the engine, a Unity Accord targeting computer running on incompatible software, and a gun barrel that was originally a sewer pipe. Fresh from the forge: clean industrial brutalism. After a campaign: covered in salvage layers, battlefield modifications, and three different repair jobs nobody remembers. Veterans literally LOOK different from fresh troops — their visual history written in scrap. It shouldn’t work. It works anyway. Mostly. Sometimes it explodes. The Horde considers this acceptable.
Visual Identity

Industrial Brutalism

Ground Forces
Massive hulking bodies wrapped in scrap metal. No two units alike — unique collages of salvaged parts from different factions and eras. Mismatched armor colors. Oversized crude weapons — slug-throwers from pipe, buzzsaws on hydraulic arms. Everything belches smoke, leaks oil, and sparks.
Air Forces
Flying scrap heaps held aloft by brute-force thrust. Jets bolted to frames, rotors on misaligned axles, open-air cockpits. Aircraft rattle, shake, and shed parts mid-flight. Not designed to be safe — designed to get close enough to drop something heavy.
Space Fleet
Ships that look like asteroids welded to engines. Hollowed-out captured asteroids with scrap-metal bulkheads and weapons bolted to every surface. Visually chaotic — no two ships share a silhouette. Structurally sound the way a mountain is.
SALVAGE COLLAGE — VISUAL PROGRESSION RULE
Because the Horde improvises and salvages everything, a veteran Scrap-Horde army is a walking museum of every faction it has fought. A Slaughter-Brute might be wearing a pristine white Unity Accord chest plate crudely welded to a dark Terran League tank tread, the whole mess painted over with burnt-orange hazard stripes that are already flaking off. You can read a Horde clan’s war history just by looking at what their armor is made of — Eternal Shards crystal shards hammered flat into shoulder guards, Revenant hull plating bent into shields, Terran League optics jury-rigged into targeting scopes. This is a visual progression indicator built into the faction: early-game Horde armies look uniform, forged from Rusthold iron. Late-game Horde armies look different because they have been eating defeated factions for an entire campaign. The scrap tells the story.

They define the cultural DNA that the game engine uses to generate names, events, dialogue, building descriptions, and emergent narrative. 21 decisions locked in Phase 2.3 lore workshop (2026-03-13). Faction reworked from ground up — mineral-based iron warriors with ork chaos-joy and dwarven iron-honor.

The Joy of War — “We fight because fighting is GLORIOUS.”
Warriors who name their guns and get genuinely upset when they break. Who argue about which explosion was better mid-battle. Who have drinking songs about famous last stands. Who consider “it only blew up twice” a glowing product review. 70% chaotic joy, 30% dwarven iron-honor. Beneath the chaos: iron-oaths that are unbreakable, Grudge Records that are never forgotten, and forge-bonds deeper than iron veins.
MINERAL BIOLOGY
Iron and calcium based lifeforms. They literally rust — oxidize, corrode, weather. “The Rust Tide” is not a metaphor. Stocky, dense, heavy — dwarven proportions with ork aggression. Consuming metal is nutrition — a battlefield is a buffet. The forge is medicine, birthplace, and temple. Working metal is working with their own biology.
GOVERNMENT
Biggest Boss + Clan Confederation. Might makes right within each clan — beat the boss, become the boss. The Horde is a confederation of competing clans, each with their own Warboss. Unity only happens when someone strong enough forces it. Game start: the Forged Construct Commander forcing unity across warring clans on Rusthold.
HOMEWORLD — RUSTHOLD
Core: Mineral-rich moon mined hollow from the inside — forge-cities in caverns, iron veins in the walls that are literally food. Surface: Ancient derelict warships captured and welded on. Orbit: Asteroid belt of smaller rocks connected by scrap-bridges and tow cables, each controlled by a different clan. Part moon, part scrapyard, part shipwreck, part fortress. Grown over centuries. Nobody planned it. Shouldn’t work. Works anyway.
MILITARY DOCTRINE
Chaos Doctrine — Every Clan Fights Different. No unified military doctrine. Skull-Splitters rush, Iron-Eaters loot, Forge-Born siege. The Commander’s challenge is orchestrating chaos into something that works — or just pointing the chaos at the enemy and hoping. Which clans you unified on Rusthold determines your army’s personality.
INTERNAL FACTIONS — 6 CLANS OF RUSTHOLD
Skull-Splitters — Rushers. Joyful violence. Melee-heavy. Headbutting as greeting.
Iron-Eaters — Looters. Mid-battle salvage. Build on the fly. Name every piece of salvage.
Forge-Born — Builders. Siege specialists. Most dwarven. Craft-pride. Insult their work at your peril.
Rust-Walkers — Survivors. Harshest territory. Scout/ranger types. Fatalistic humor. “Already rusting.”
Slag-Kings — Rulers. Richest clan. Control best mineral veins. Political by Horde standards. “Everything has a price.”
Grind-Teeth — Berserkers. Battle-rage extreme. Quiet between battles. DEAFENING during. The silence is scarier than the noise.
RELIGION — THE FORGE + THE ENGINE
The Forge is the temple — fire transforms ore into warrior. The act of forging IS prayer. Master smiths are priests. The Engine is the mythologized force behind it all — the thing that lives in metal, the process of creation through destruction. “The Engine spoke. You came out.” Aethyn relics trigger rage because they resonate with iron blood — feels like the forge calling from somewhere ancient. The Iron Tide: collective war-frenzy through mineral resonance — iron calls to iron. The more warriors fighting, the harder each one hits. The battlefield hums at a frequency only they can feel.
FOUNDING CRISIS — THE FIRST FIRING
The Aethyn relic activated deep in Rusthold’s mineral core. The ancestors followed the vibration down — deeper and deeper through the iron veins. Found the relic at the center. Built a forge around it. Shoved it in a furnace. The energy surge supercharged everything — the First Firing forged the first true Horde warriors from raw mineral ore. The First Forge still sits around that relic at Rusthold’s deepest point. The iron that feeds the entire Horde flows through an Aethyn Strategist fragment they’ve been using as a smelter for millennia. Their most sacred place is a cosmic signal beacon repurposed as an industrial tool.
NAMING CONVENTIONS
Personal: Full Clan-Title-Name — “Forge-Born Slag-Hammer Krag.” Nobody uses the full thing. In battle it’s just “KRAG!” Harsh, short, metal sounds: Krag, Boltz, Grenn, Tharr, Skarn, Drukk. Weapons: Named for what they did — “Loud Problem,” “The One That Worked,” “Regret.” Ships: “Big Ugly,” “Mostly Intact,” “Shouldn’t Float.”
POST-FRACTURE HISTORY — THE BREAKING
The Warrior Caste was fighting the Vorax when the Fracture hit — mid-battle. The psychic shock fused their mineral biology into something cruder, tougher, angrier. They didn’t slowly forget — they were BROKEN and rebuilt themselves from scrap. The first Horde warriors were shattered soldiers who welded themselves back together and kept fighting. Origin through trauma: the Horde is what happens when warriors shatter and refuse to stop.
XENOPHOBIA — MATERIAL HIERARCHY + COMBAT OVERRIDE
Default: iron > flesh. Mineral races superior to biological. But: beat a Horde warrior in combat and the hierarchy resets for you. “You’re made of meat but you fight like iron. You’re alright.” Terrans: “Soft. Brave, but soft.” Shards: “Pretty glass. Don’t squeeze.” The Revenant: “At least they build with metal.” Unity Accord: “Talk talk talk. Build something or shut up.”
ECONOMY — SCRAP + FAVORS
Physical things trade in scrap — weight of usable metal. Labor and loyalty trade in favors. Two parallel systems: Slag-Kings are rich in scrap, Skull-Splitters are rich in combat-favors. Both have power, different kinds.
SOCIAL STRUCTURE — FORGE BY DAY, ARENA BY NIGHT
Build things during the day, break things (and each other) at night. The forge is workplace, social hub, temple, and hospital. The arena is entertainment, dispute resolution, and promotion pathway. Celebrity fight commentators are as respected as good fighters. Best nights: someone brings a new weapon to the arena. “I built this today. Let me show you what it does.”
TECH AESTHETIC — BRUTAL NEW, LAYERED VETERANS
Fresh from the forge: clean industrial brutalism. Exposed gears, visible weld seams, bolts the size of fists. After campaigns: covered in salvage layers, repairs, battlefield modifications. Veterans literally LOOK different than fresh troops — visual history written in scrap. Vehicles are asymmetrical Frankenstein machines — part Horde, part salvaged enemy tech, part mystery metal nobody remembers installing.

ARCHITECT — THE SMITH
Quiet builder. Forge-Born. Thinks in decades. “Let me build it right.” Represents the Builder axis — craft-pride, patience, infrastructure. Will never rush. Will never build something shoddy. The answer to every problem is a better forge.
VANGUARD — THE FIST
Skull-Splitter incarnate. Lives for the charge. Every problem looks like something to headbutt. But genuine warmth — fights for forge-brothers, not glory. The Iron Tide personified. “FIGHT THEM!” is both battle cry and policy recommendation.
TYRANT — THE RUST
Old. Corroded. Barely holding together. Remembers everything. Rust-Walker who’s seen too much. “Burn it all. Start over. That’s what iron does.” Represents the Breaker axis — destruction as renewal. The Grudge Record keeper. Every wrong carved in metal.
Faction Bible — AI Director Seeds

Procedural Generation DNA

VORAX ADAPTATION — ARMORED SCRAP-SWARM
Vorax that consumed Horde warriors gain iron plating — heavier, tougher, slower. AND they start improvising with salvage — grafting scrap onto themselves mid-battle. The swarm that innovates. Rust-colored chitin with welded debris armor. Recognizable Horde weapons on alien flesh.
NEGOTIATION AT THE NEXUS
Surface: Confusion → Respect. “You’re a MACHINE that FIGHTS? ...I like you.” Underneath: The Forged Construct Commander feels something in the Guardian systems — like the First Forge, like The Engine. Iron calling to crystal. Recognition through material, not mind.
FIRST CONTACT DOCTRINE
Player choice moment — advisors disagree, Commander decides. The Fist: “FIGHT THEM!” The Smith: “Wait. Watch. Learn what they build.” The Rust: “They’re in our space. Remove them.”
CASCADE VULNERABILITY — 3 PRESSURE POINTS
Resource Starvation: Cut the iron supply → can’t forge, repair, or grow. Mineral bodies need mineral fuel. But the inverse is lethal: fighting the Horde in a wreckage-filled, ruin-dense system is suicide — every destroyed ship and collapsed structure is a meal. They feed on the battlefield itself and grow stronger with every exchange. The only way to break a Horde invasion is to fight them in empty, barren systems where there is nothing to scavenge, nothing to consume, nothing to weld back together. Deny them raw material and the warriors begin to rust. Starve the forge, and the warriors rust.
Clan Fracture: Remove or weaken the boss → clans splinter → internal war. The confederation held together by force alone.
Forge Destruction: Kill the fire → can’t produce, repair, or heal → rusting warriors with no salvation.
DIVERGENCE — TWO AXES
Order ↔ Chaos: How unified are the clans? Standardized army vs. wild chaos.
Builder ↔ Breaker: Create or destroy? Forge-Born influence vs. Skull-Splitter influence.
Tyrant Horde: Ordered breakers — a disciplined war machine that eats everything.
Unifier Horde: Chaotic builders — joyful creators who build wild, impossible things alongside everyone.
