THE DUAL THREAT MODEL
Ring + Wall = Funnel · Weapon Asymmetry — Chapter 31
VORAX = THE CLOSING RING (The Clock)
Expanding inward from the galaxy's edge. Consuming systems. Accelerating. THE TIMER. Cannot negotiate. Cannot coexist with. Only options: slow (destroy Tendrils) or stop permanently (activate Nexus). See Ch. 12 for Vorax spawn/movement prototype values.
GUARDIANS = THE WALL (The Destination)
Defending the galactic center. Stationary. Overwhelmingly powerful. Between you and the Nexus. Two approaches: fight through (Conquest Path — kinetic loadouts) or convince (Unification Path — 75% Compendium + 3+ alliances + negotiate the Warden-Commander).
WEAPON ASYMMETRY — THE CORE STRATEGIC DILEMMA
Anti-Vorax: Energy weapons (plasma, laser, ion). Kinetic works but doesn't exploit weakness. Biotic units resist energy.
Anti-Guardian: Kinetic weapons (railgun, ballistic). Energy weapons HEAL Guardian constructs — a hard counter that cannot be ignored.
The Dilemma: An army optimized for one threat is weak against the other. Three responses:
· Maintain two armies (expensive, complex)
· Re-equip between fronts (slow, risky if both active)
· Research Legendary hybrid weapons (late-game only — The Keystone)
The Funnel Structure

Ring Closes → Player Pushed Toward Guardian Wall

The galaxy map is deliberately designed so that as the Vorax ring closes inward, the only safe territory remaining is progressively closer to the galactic core — where the Guardians are strongest. The player can never simply avoid both threats indefinitely. The map architecture forces a confrontation with the wall while the clock is still running.
