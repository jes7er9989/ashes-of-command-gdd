The Original Civilization
The Aethyn — Progenitors of All Factions
THE AETHYN
A single unified species — psychically connected as one mind, one signal. Galaxy-spanning utopia at their peak, with intergalactic warp lanes connecting to other galaxies. Technology was psychic-crystalline hybrid — mental energy channeled through crystal matrices. Mind = software, crystals = hardware. They manufactured entire galaxies, built Dyson spheres routinely, and existed tens of thousands of years ago — so far back they're not even myth to modern factions. The modern factions are children playing in the ruins of gods.

The Cataclysm
The Fracture — The Desperate Choice
THE VORAX CRISIS
The Aethyn's unified psychic signal acted as a beacon — a lighthouse burning across the void. It attracted the Vorax, extragalactic predators that had always existed, always hungered, drawn irresistibly to psychic civilizations.
They targeted this galaxy and cut the warp lanes — sealing every exit. The Aethyn in this galaxy were completely isolated from the greater civilization. No reinforcements. No escape. No contact with the thousands of other galaxies they had once called home.
A centuries-long war followed. The Aethyn fought hard — a civilization that manufactured galaxies turned its full might against the invaders — but they were losing. Slowly. Inevitably. The Vorax consumed everything.
THE DESPERATE CHOICE
The entire species chose. All castes. All minds. Billions voting yes to their own destruction.
The last act of unity was choosing to end unity.
Full knowledge. They knew exactly what the Reclamation Engine would do. Every mind understood. Every caste agreed. Eyes open. No deception, no coercion — the most democratic act in galactic history was a vote for annihilation.
The Engine had been carefully designed over decades — not a rushed prototype born of panic, but a deliberate instrument of last resort. They had time to build it right. They just wished they never had to use it.
THE RECLAMATION ENGINE
True name: The Reclamation Engine — only discovered through gameplay. Known as "The Core" until its true name is revealed.
Located inside the Nexus system (sys-nexus). The Nexus is the location; the Reclamation Engine is the device within it. Physically housed at the center of “The Scar” — Core system planet 8 — surrounded by the warped space-time it created when it fired. The Scar’s environmental hazards make the Engine nearly impossible to reach.
Dual effect: shattered the Aethyn into scattered seeds AND purged all Vorax from the galaxy simultaneously.
A wave of energy blasted from the Core — ripping apart everything at the molecular level. It traveled outward in all directions, through every system, every arm, every void between stars. One wave. One moment. Everything changed.
THE AFTERMATH
Vorax: Erased completely. Zero traces. Zero evidence they ever existed. Wiped so thoroughly that no trace or myth remains.
Aethyn: Shattered but not destroyed — scattered as seeds to distant systems. Fragments of a god-species, carrying broken genes and fading echoes of what they once were.
Technology: Mostly destroyed. Surviving pieces became the relics — crystalline structures that still respond to psychic proximity, sleeping and waiting.
Warp Lanes: Burned out. Overloaded by the energy surge. The highways between galaxies — gone.
The Galaxy: Scarred. Warped regions, dead zones, anomalous nebulae — the wounds of a weapon that saved everything by destroying everything.

The Aftermath
The Scattering — Seeds Among the Stars
HOW FRAGMENTS LANDED
During the centuries-long war, Aethyn populations were already distributed across the galaxy — deployed, displaced, fighting on a thousand fronts. The wave hit them where they already were.
Randomness determined who survived. A cosmic lottery. Some fragments landed on habitable worlds. Some didn't. The five factions are the ones that survived — countless others were not so fortunate.
Proximity to intact Aethyn infrastructure determined the head start. Groups near shielded ruins had materials, shelter, maybe functioning tech. Groups on barren worlds started with almost nothing. Groups near crystal deposits retained more psychic residue. Groups near AI facilities got adopted by machines.
UNEQUAL DISTRIBUTION — WHAT EACH FACTION RETAINED
ETERNAL SHARDS
Retained most psychic ability. Scholar Caste descendants landed near crystal deposits, preserving the strongest connection to the old resonance. Active psychic abilities, crystal resonance, can sense echoes of what was.
NECRO-LEGION
Retained nothing biological, but landed near intact Aethyn AI infrastructure. Not descended from a specific caste — adopted by machines. The AI recognized descendant DNA and began rebuilding.
TERRAN LEAGUE
Got almost nothing. Near-clean slate. Engineer Caste descendants who rebuilt through sheer determination and ingenuity. What they lack in inheritance they make up for in stubborn refusal to stay broken.
SCRAP-HORDE
Got numbers but nothing else. Warrior Caste descendants dumped in a hostile environment. Survived through aggression, resourcefulness, and an instinct for violence that runs deeper than memory.
UNITY ACCORD
Got moderate everything. Shepherd Caste descendants — enough fragments landed in proximity to find each other. Their instinct for unity persisted even when the memory of unity did not.

RECOVERY TIMELINE
Recovery varies wildly by faction. Factions near Aethyn ruins leapfrogged through reverse-engineering. Some have been spacefaring for millennia; others cracked orbit recently. This creates natural power imbalances and resentment — the seeds of every faction war in the game.
THE BURIED TRUTH
No faction knows they share a common ancestor. The truth is completely buried — not hidden, not suppressed, simply gone. Tens of thousands of years erased every trace.
Zero memory of the Vorax. The Reclamation Engine wiped them so thoroughly that no trace, no myth, no bedtime story remains. The greatest threat the galaxy ever faced left no footprint.
Every faction believes they're indigenous to their homeworld. The truth rewriting identity is a MASSIVE narrative beat — the moment the player realizes these warring peoples are family.

Two-Phase Structure
The Crucible (Simulation) → The Final War (Reality)
THE CRUCIBLE (SIMULATION)
Standard random procedural galaxy each run. Death = blackness → re-decanting → new run.
Advisors are "helpful AI voices" (or so the player assumes). No permadeath. Archive accumulates.
Procedural "Artifacts" — fragments of the Original Strategist's memories. Emotionally charged but mechanically optional.
Simulation is PSYCHOLOGICALLY IDENTICAL to reality. No wireframe filter, no "this isn't real" cues. First run has NO HUD watermark — completely clean. Watermark appears on subsequent runs after the reveal.
THE FINAL WAR (REALITY)
Procedural Destiny galaxy shaped by cumulative Alignment. Permadeath active (Standard/Hardcore).
Advisors revealed as REAL PEOPLE. Faces, physical presence, personal stakes. Everything they said was sincere.
Meet faction leaders in person. Hybrid cinematic → dialogue sequences.
Two paths to the Core: Conquest (destroy the Guardians) or Unification (convince them).
THE WAKE-UP
Delivered by whichever advisor has the best relationship with the Commander (alignment-dependent — see Ch.23). Valerius is the canonical Terran/Architect version. Player CHOOSES when to wake up. Must WIN a simulation run after recommendation. Waking underprepared = brutally difficult Final War. Continuing to prepare = more Archive advantages, but the Vorax won't wait. Transition: simulation glitch cutscene → the galaxy is real → Map Phase of the Final War.
THE REVEAL — FIRST PLAYTHROUGH
First run has NO FRAME. Player doesn't know it's a simulation. No tutorial popup, no "Welcome to the Crucible." Just: wake up, here's a galaxy, go.
Reveal happens after first complete game (win OR lose). "SIMULATION COMPLETE" moment. Everything recontextualizes.
No HUD watermark on first run. Completely clean. Watermark appears on all subsequent runs.
Custom Mode: Selectable difficulty tier from the start — player controls run limits, AI strength, Vorax timing, saves, and permadeath.

A Consciousness Born From Fragments

Aethyn Relics & Strategist Fragments
Artifacts System
Relics are found on planets — chance-based, density varies by proximity to old Aethyn core worlds. They provide: 🔬 Research Points (accelerate R&D), 📖 Compendium Entries (data fragments, star charts), 💬 Advisor Dialogue (each advisor reacts uniquely — 3 perspectives per find), and 🧠 Strategist Memory Fragments (the rare, important ones that build sentience). Dyson Spheres are ultra-rare (~15–25% chance per map) and function as map-defining strategic objectives.
The player's Commander type changes how Artifacts are experienced — Bio Clones feel inherited memories, Psychic Echoes experience bleeding dreams, Forged Constructs hallucinate data fragments, Reactivated Cores read archaeological data, Military AIs encounter anomalous system logs. Mixed emotional valence: some warm, some devastating.
Five Endings
Named Outcomes by Alignment Path + Pyrrhic
ARCHITECT — "THE ORDERED GALAXY"
Convince the Guardians through knowledge and diplomacy. Cooperative resonance imposed on the galaxy. Safe, stable — but freedom sacrificed. The Guardians provide knowledge instead of soldiers. Aethyn archives unlocked.
VANGUARD — "THE SCORCHED GALAXY"
Destroy the Guardians. Take the Core by force. Maximum power — Vorax annihilated but galaxy devastated. The chain reaction of Guardian deaths reshapes the core systems. Partial Guardian restoration at best — allies on THEIR terms.
TYRANT — "THE IRON THRONE"
Commander seizes absolute power. Vorax subjugated, not destroyed. Galactic deity. The Guardians refuse or are corrupted — unstable, turning on your forces. The Tyrant ending is them being heartbroken, not stubborn.
SECRET: UNIFIER — "THE RESONANCE"
The species-split reversed. Galaxy healed. Vorax sealed permanently. Warp lanes reopened to other galaxies. Requires: balanced alignment + complete Unification negotiation + full Artifact chain. Full Guardian restoration — fight side by side. Reveals the Strategist’s true name. The golden ending.
PYRRHIC VICTORY — 5TH ENDING
A distinct ending, not a variant. Win the Final War at catastrophic cost — galaxy saved, army gone, rebuilt from nothing. The price of victory paid in full. Own cinematic, own emotional beat. Can result from any alignment path. The player won, but the cost makes them question if it was worth it.
