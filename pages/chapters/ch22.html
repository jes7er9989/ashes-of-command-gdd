CHAIN OF COMMAND
Rank System · General AI Ego · Personality Traits · Standing Orders — Chapters 22-23
Units grow from anonymous Rookies to autonomous Generals through a kill-count promotion chain. At Elite rank they develop personality traits. At Commander they gain auras and CP abilities. At General they can command entire sectors and fleets independently — becoming characters in their own right, with goals, opinions, and defection risk.

General AI Ego System

Loyalty · Friction · Defection Arc

LOYALTY SCORE (0–100)
Gains: Victories, alignment with trait, respected orders, gifts of equipment, acknowledgment in Council.
Loses: Orders ignored, orders that contradict trait, excessive casualties under their command, rivals promoted over them.
<20 Loyalty: Friction events trigger. <5: Defection risk active.
FRICTION EVENTS
Disagreement: General pushes back in War Council. Requires player acknowledgment.
Insubordination: Modifies standing order 10-20% toward their trait preference.
Challenge: Demands resource, territory, or recognition — binary choice with consequences.
DEFECTION OUTCOMES BY TRAIT
Aggressive → Joins the faction you're currently at war with. Turns their tactical knowledge against you. · Cautious → Surrenders peacefully to nearest neutral faction. Takes their equipment but no violence. · Loyal → Vanishes heartbroken. Very rare. Returns as event during Final War. · Ambitious → Joins the strongest enemy faction. Accelerates their veteran production. · Reckless → Charges Vorax tendril or Guardian fleet alone. Dies heroically on their own terms. · Cunning → Goes fully independent. Becomes Pirate Warlord — minor faction on the galaxy map.
