Planetary Weather System

Predictable from Orbit, Sudden on the Ground

Every planet in the galaxy has active weather systems that affect ground combat. Weather is partially predictable — orbital scans before invasion reveal current conditions and short-term forecasts — but sudden shifts can occur mid-battle. Commanders must decide whether to wait for favorable conditions, attack during a storm for concealment, or accept the weather as another variable in an already complex equation. Weather events vary by planet type and affect visibility, movement, damage, equipment performance, and morale.
WEATHER INTEL — TWO-TIER SYSTEM
PRE-INVASION INTEL
Before committing ground forces, orbital scans show current weather and a 1-cycle forecast. Players can see “Blizzard active on Ice World” and choose to wait or attack.
MID-COMBAT SHIFTS
Some weather events are sudden (sandstorms, eruptions). These trigger during ground combat with diegetic warnings — staged alarm sequences that escalate through visual, audio, and environmental channels (see Diegetic Warning Framework below).
WEATHER CYCLING
Planets cycle through weather states over time. Long campaigns experience multiple weather shifts.
VISIBILITY & MOVEMENT EFFECTS
Fog/Mist: Reduces sightline range. Infantry stealth bonus. Ranged units penalized.
Storms (Rain/Snow/Sand): Reduce visibility AND movement speed. Equipment degradation on prolonged exposure PH.
Clear Skies: Maximum sightlines. Ranged units and air support at full effectiveness.
Blizzard/Whiteout: Near-zero visibility. Point-blank combat only. Movement severely restricted.
COMBAT & EQUIPMENT EFFECTS
Sandstorms: Reduce range, degrade shields, jam sensors. Provide concealment for repositioning.
Heatwaves: Biological units take stamina debuffs. Mechanical units unaffected. Coolant systems stressed PH.
Electrical Storms: Disable or degrade electronic targeting systems. EMP-like effect on tech-heavy units.
Tidal Events: Flood low-ground territories temporarily. Force units to high ground or take damage.

Megastructure Hazards

Catastrophic System Failures

Megastructures do not experience weather. They suffer Catastrophic System Failures. Catastrophic System Failures are structural, violent, and often irreversible.

Diegetic Warning Framework

The Atmosphere Should Break Before the Event Hits

“The player should not just watch a timer tick down; they should feel the atmosphere breaking.” — Generic HUD countdowns are replaced with diegetic tactical alarms. The command console reacts to in-world conditions: visual banners, audio buildups, and environmental particle effects telegraph incoming events through the game world itself rather than through an overlay timer. Basic consoles show alarms only — numeric countdowns require the Advanced Meteorological Sensors tech unlock.
DIEGETIC WARNING DESIGN RULES
RULE 1 — TECH-GATED TIMER VISIBILITY
Basic command console shows alarm stages only — no numeric digits. Unlocking the Advanced Meteorological Sensors tech node adds precise numeric countdowns to all warning banners. Parallels the tech-gated intel curve in Containment Breach (see §13 Containment Breach Rule 6). PH Advanced Meteorological Sensors tech node placement pending tech tree pass.
RULE 2 — TRIPLE-CHANNEL ALARM STACK
Every warning fires across three simultaneous channels:
Visual: Banner text, UI border flicker, color shifts.
Audio: Wind/static ambient buildup, alarm chime, crescendo stingers.
Environmental: In-world particle effects before impact (dust, sparks, static, debris).
No camera shake — preserves long-match comfort.
RULE 3 — VARIABLE STAGES BY SEVERITY
Instakill-class events: 3 stages (slow buildup, long lead time).
Lethal DoT events: 2 stages (medium warning).
Minor events: 1 stage (quick flash).
Stage count IS the severity grammar — players learn that more stages mean more danger. Directly implements CSF Rule 4 (Warning Lead Time Scales with Severity). PH Per-severity-tier exact stage timings pending balance pass.
RULE 4 — HYBRID ALARM GRAMMAR
Every alarm banner uses the format: [TECHNICAL READOUT] — actionable consequence.
Technical instrument language on top; short, plain-language tactical subtext below.
Example: [BAROMETRIC DROP DETECTED] — Storm imminent, prepare for reduced sightlines
RULE 5 — FRAMEWORK SCOPE
Applies to: Weather events, Catastrophic System Failures, combat events (enemy reinforcements, air strikes, artillery, orbital bombardment).
Excluded: Strategic-map events (Vorax migration, faction declarations) — different scale and language. PH Full combat event type list in scope pending combat pass.

See Before You Commit

Before committing ground forces to a planet, commanders use a tiered scanning system to reveal planetary details — from basic surface composition visible on approach, to deep subsurface scans that require specialized technology and ground presence. Better intel means better preparation. The difference between a successful invasion and a catastrophic ambush is often one deep scan.

Revenant Sleeper Caches

The Sleeping Army Beneath Your Feet

Scattered across the galaxy, buried beneath the surface of countless worlds, the Revenant left behind sleeper caches — dormant soldiers sealed in cryo-preservation during the Long Sleep. These caches exist on nearly every planet type, varying in size, condition, and accessibility. For the Revenant player, finding and waking sleepers is not optional; it is the PRIMARY method of army expansion. The Revenant does not build new units — they excavate and reactivate their ancient forces. For every other faction, sleeper caches are a threat, a resource, or both.

SLEEPER STATE SYSTEM
The condition of found sleepers depends on a COMBINATION of factors. No single variable determines what you find — every cache is a confluence of time, environment, luck, and history.
TIME EXPOSURE
How long the cache has been exposed to environmental conditions. Caches on stable worlds (Ice, Dead) tend to be better preserved than volatile worlds (Volcanic, Jungle).
PLANET TRAITS
Certain planetary traits improve or degrade sleeper condition. Cryo-Vault trait = pristine preservation. Hazardous traits = degraded systems.
RANDOM VARIATION
Even within the same world, some caches are pristine while others are corrupted or empty. Every cache is a roll.
PRIOR DISTURBANCE
If other factions have been on the planet, they may have damaged, looted, or inadvertently destroyed sleeper caches. First arrival advantage.
PRISTINE
Full functionality. Unit wakes at 100% capability. Rare. Most common on Ancient Ruins and Ice Worlds with Cryo-Vault trait.
OPERATIONAL
Minor degradation. Unit wakes at 80–95% capability. Frame transfer corruption is minimal. Most common result.
DEGRADED
Significant wear. Unit wakes at 50–79% capability. May have missing equipment, corrupted memory, or damaged subsystems. Requires repair investment.
CORRUPTED
Severely damaged. Unit may wake hostile (attacks all factions), non-functional, or with critical system failures. Dangerous to activate. May be salvageable for parts only.
THE WAKE MECHANIC
Waking a sleeper is not instant. Each cache requires POWER allocation and TIME to reactivate.
Power Cost: Scales with cache size and condition PH
Wake Time: Scales with cache size — small cache = 1 cycle, large cache = 3–5 cycles PH
Priority Choice: Player chooses: wake a few elite units fast, or mass-activate basic Legionnaires slowly
Opportunity Cost: Power diverted to waking sleepers is power NOT available for other infrastructure
“The Revenant’s strategic dilemma: how much of your energy budget do you spend waking the past versus powering the present?”
FACTION INTERACTION WITH SLEEPER CACHES
REVENANT PLAYER
Full access. Can interface with cache systems, assess contents before waking, choose which units to prioritize. Faster wake times. Can wake units that other factions cannot.
OTHER FACTIONS
Can detect caches with Deep Scan. Faces a binary choice: Safe Purge (heavy resource cost, neutralizes the cache cleanly) or Quick Crack (fast excavation for Aethyn technology, risks a Containment Breach). Can also attempt to destroy caches outright (denying Revenant recruitment). Cannot wake sleepers — the technology is Revenant-specific. See Containment Breach below for full rules.
STRATEGIC DENIAL
Smart commanders who know the Revenant is in the game will prioritize destroying sleeper caches on planets they control — denying the Revenant their primary recruitment pipeline.
Containment Breach Protocol

Sleeper Cache Risk Assessment

CONTAINMENT BREACH
When a non-Revenant faction deep-scans a Sleeper Cache, they face an explicit binary choice. There is no third option. You are literally fighting a tomb to steal its jewelry.
SAFE PURGE
Heavy resource investment to neutralize the cache cleanly before opening. The cache is rendered inert — no Revenant units awaken, no breach occurs. Contents are safely extracted.

Cost: Scales with cache tier (T1/T2/T3) PH
Duration: Commitment cycles required during purge PH
Result: Safe extraction, no combat risk
QUICK CRACK
Fast excavation targeting Aethyn technology inside the cache. Minimal resource cost, maximum risk. A failed containment triggers a Containment Breach — degraded Revenant units awaken and instantly initiate a ground battle on the node.

Cost: Minimal (excavation crew only)
Duration: Immediate (this turn)
Risk: Containment Breach — ground battle on the node

Planetary Re-Origination

Reshaping Worlds to Serve the Empire

Planetary Re-Origination — also known as Biosphere Forging — is the violent, galactic-scale act of tearing a Dead World’s crust apart and force-feeding it atmosphere over 15–30 cycles. The process generates thermal and electromagnetic noise on a galactic scale, lighting the world up like a bonfire on the map for the full duration. You aren’t just building a world; you are ringing a dinner bell. Dead Worlds can be converted into entirely new planet types. Other worlds can be enhanced but not fundamentally changed. Re-Origination is expensive, slow, and only worth the investment on strategically positioned worlds that justify the cost. It is the ultimate expression of the front-loading philosophy: suffer now, benefit forever.
DEAD WORLD CONVERSION
Dead Worlds can be re-originated into a specific planet type chosen by the player.
Available Target Types: Terrestrial · Desert · Ice World · Jungle · Ocean PH — not all types may be available
Process: Multi-stage transformation PH — expands on the Vorax Recovery stage framework (see above)
Cost: High upfront investment + ongoing per-cycle maintenance during transformation PH
Timeline: 15–30 cycles depending on target type PH
Result: Fully functional planet of the chosen type with standard traits and yields
“The ultimate long-term investment. A Dead World on a critical warp lane becomes whatever you need it to be.”
WORLD ENHANCEMENT
Non-Dead worlds can be enhanced but not type-changed.
Enhancements: Yield boost · Add building slots · Improve planetary traits · Remove negative traits
Investment: Smaller than full conversion — targeted improvements rather than wholesale transformation
Limitation: Cannot change a Desert into a Jungle — can make a Desert produce more
“Polishing a diamond vs carving a new one.”

Moon Generation & Typing

Not Just Dead Rocks

Moons are not a single planet type — they are a SIZE class that can carry any biome. A moon orbiting a gas giant might be a frozen ice moon, a volcanic hellscape heated by tidal forces, or a jungle-covered world with its own atmosphere. Moon type is influenced by its parent planet and the host star system, creating realistic orbital environments. Bigger moons around gas giants can function as small planets with meaningful resource yields.

PARENT PLANET INFLUENCE
Gas Giant moons: Higher chance of Ice, Volcanic (tidal heating), or Dead. Larger average size (up to small-planet scale).
Terrestrial moons: Tend toward Dead or Ice. Smaller average size.
Volcanic planet moons: Higher Volcanic/Dead chance (system-wide geological activity).
Ocean world moons: Higher Ice chance (tidal water cycling).
Capital World moons: Higher chance of matching parent biome or Dead (strip-mined for resources during civilization building).
“The parent shapes the child. A gas giant’s gravitational forces create volcanic moons. A frozen system breeds frozen satellites.”
SYSTEM TYPE INFLUENCE
Hot star systems (Blue Giant, Red Giant): More Volcanic and Desert moons.
Cool star systems (Red Dwarf, White Dwarf): More Ice and Dead moons.
Stable star systems (Yellow Main Sequence): Widest variety — any moon type possible.
Binary systems: Unstable orbits create more Dead and Volcanic moons.
“The star sets the temperature range. The parent sets the geology. Random variation fills in the rest.”

Tiny. Barely worth landing on. No atmosphere. Quick skirmish or sensor outpost only.
Always Dead type. Low gravity extreme (+30% speed, +40% range).
Resource: negligible. Strategic: sensor/staging only.
STANDARD MOON
3–5 TERRITORIES
The typical moon. Can carry any biome type. Low gravity guaranteed.
This is the Moon entry in the planet catalog — most moons are this size.
Quick fights, forward operating bases, staging areas.
MAJOR MOON
6–10 TERRITORIES
Gas giant satellites primarily. Functions like a small planet.
Can have meaningful resource yields, settlements, and infrastructure.
May be the most valuable body in a gas giant system (since the giant itself only has platforms).
“Some moons are bigger than other factions’ homeworlds.”
GENERATION RULES
Every planet has a CHANCE to generate 0–3 moons PH
Gas Giants generate 1–5 moons (always at least 1, higher average)
Moon type rolled from weighted probability table based on parent + system
Moons inherit low gravity modifier regardless of type
Named moons use procedural generation (parent name + suffix)
“In a Large galaxy, expect 30–50 moons. Most are forgettable. A few will decide the war.”
