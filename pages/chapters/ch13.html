PLANETARY BODIES & BIOMES
17 Planet Types · 5 Megastructures · Variable Territories · Weather · Scanning — Chapter 13
Every planet in the galaxy is assigned one of 17 types — 12 standard worlds and 5 rare megastructures — that determines visual appearance, terrain palette for ground battles, territory count, base resource yield, environmental hazards, and planetary trait probabilities. Planet types are assigned during galaxy generation based on host star type with weighted random variation. A global weather system affects all planet types, and a tiered scanning mechanic reveals planetary details before committing ground forces.

CAPITAL WORLD
8–22 Territories · Fixed Per Faction · Unique Terrain
Dense urban sprawl dominates every Capital World — multi-story buildings provide garrison positions with interlocking fields of fire, wide avenues funnel vehicles into kill zones while offering assault corridors, elevated overpasses and mag-rail bridges create critical height advantage positions, and underground tunnel networks allow infantry flanking routes beneath the surface. Destructible environments are key: buildings collapse under sustained fire, creating rubble cover that reshapes the battlefield mid-engagement. Expect fortified government districts, industrial sectors with heavy machinery cover, civilian zones with narrow alleys, and orbital defense battery emplacements that can be captured and turned against attackers.
From orbit, a Capital World blazes with light — city grids cover 40%+ of the surface in geometric patterns of civilization. Orbital rings of defense platforms, shipyards, and commercial stations form a visible halo. The atmosphere carries a permanent industrial haze, punctuated by the glow of massive energy reactors. Approaching fleets see a world wrapped in the infrastructure of empire — comm-sat constellations, debris fields from centuries of orbital construction, and the ever-present patrol routes of defense flotillas.
STRATEGIC VALUE
- Fixed territory count per faction (PH values pending balance)
- Starting infrastructure scales with difficulty setting and faction choice
- Defender’s advantage: auto-militia, orbital defense batteries, morale bonus
- Capital tag is permanent — losing it triggers empire-wide morale crisis
- Recapture is a strategic priority, not optional

TERRESTRIAL
10–14 Territories · 40% Encounter Rate · Subtypes: Temperate, Arid-Temperate, Tropical
The most tactically balanced terrain in the galaxy — open grasslands provide clean sightlines for ranged engagements, scattered forests offer light cover and concealment for ambush tactics, river crossings create natural chokepoints that reward defensive positioning, and rolling hills provide moderate height advantage without the extreme verticality of mountain worlds. Mixed terrain forces diverse army compositions: pure infantry struggles in open fields, pure vehicles bog down in forests, and balanced forces dominate. Weather patterns shift between clear skies (favoring ranged), rain (reducing visibility), and fog banks (infantry ambush conditions).
Earth-like beauty from orbit — deep blue oceans covering 60–70% of the surface, green and brown continental landmasses with visible mountain ranges, white cloud formations tracking weather systems. These are the worlds every faction recognizes as ‘home’ regardless of origin. Scattered city lights on the night side reveal moderate population density. Visible agricultural patterns on temperate continents.
STRATEGIC VALUE
- 40% random encounter chance per cycle — the galaxy’s most contested real estate
- Balanced resource yield: universally valuable, no faction gains disproportionate advantage
- Soft advantages for factions whose biology or technology suits temperate environments
- Additional strategic upsides pending balance pass PH
- The ‘standard’ planet against which all other combat modifiers are measured
SUBTYPES
- Temperate: Balanced biomes, moderate weather, classic mixed-terrain battlefields
- Arid-Temperate: Drier climate, sparse vegetation, longer sightlines, more open terrain
- Tropical: Dense coastal vegetation, frequent storms, humidity affects electronics and visibility

VOLCANIC
8–12 Territories · Event-Driven Hazards · Geothermal Energy
A hellscape of obsidian ridges providing hard cover that shatters under heavy weapons fire, static lava flows creating impassable channels across the battlefield, volcanic vents that pulse with AOE damage during eruption events PH, and ash clouds that reduce accuracy during active eruptions. The ground itself is unstable — seismic tremors can collapse tunnel systems and shift terrain elevation mid-battle. Random eruption events redirect molten rivers and reshape the battlefield dynamically. Environmental damage comes from events (eruptions, vent bursts, lava flow shifts), not passive atmospheric effects.
An angry world of orange-red surface glow visible from deep space — active lava flows trace luminous arterial patterns across the dark volcanic surface. Mountain chains of active volcanoes belch columns of ash into a thick, choking atmosphere. The sky is a permanent twilight of sulfurous haze punctuated by the red glow of eruptions. From orbit, the planet pulses like a burning coal, its surface a constantly shifting mosaic of cooling obsidian and fresh magma.
COMBAT ENVIRONMENT
- Environmental damage from events (eruptions, vent bursts, lava flow shifts) — not passive
- Static lava flows = impassable terrain that channels movement
- Random eruption events dynamically reshape the battlefield
- Both static hazards (lava channels) and dynamic hazards (eruptions, tremors)
- Ash clouds reduce accuracy during active eruption events
RESOURCE PROFILE
- Highest Scrap yield of any planet type (5⚙ base)
- Geothermal energy bonus (+E yield) PH
- Zero Intel and zero Influence — a resource world, not a cultural hub
- Normal building capacity plus specialized slots (Geothermal Plant)
- Niche industrial world: raw material output, nothing else

OCEAN / ARCHIPELAGO
8–12 Territories · Island Warfare · Mixed Transport
Island warfare defines every engagement — landmasses of varying size connected by bridges, causeways, and shallow fords create a patchwork of defensible positions separated by water. Some islands are large enough to support full vehicle deployment and combined-arms operations. Movement between islands uses drop pods, transport ships, or bridges and causeways depending on what the map and player have available. Beach chokepoints funnel attackers into pre-sighted kill zones. Dense tropical interiors provide concealment once past the beaches. Periodic tidal flooding events submerge low-lying territories, forcing units to higher ground.
A water world — 90%+ ocean surface with scattered island chains and atolls visible from orbit like scattered emeralds on deep blue silk. Planet-spanning tropical storm systems track across the ocean, their spiral arms covering entire hemispheres. Shallow reefs create turquoise halos around island groups. The few settlements cluster on the largest islands, their structures built to withstand the constant storms. From orbit, the planet appears almost entirely blue, broken only by the white of cloud systems and the dark green of island vegetation.
COMBAT ENVIRONMENT
- Island territories with varying sizes — some infantry-only, some support vehicles
- Vehicles allowed on larger islands for combined-arms operations
- Inter-island movement depends on player assets: drop pods, transport ships, bridges/causeways
- No water-specific naval units — transport capability is king
- Beach chokepoints and dense tropical interiors shape every assault
RESOURCE PROFILE
- Intel-rich world — 15% chance of Flux Deposit trait (+3 Intel/cycle)
- Niche resource value: specialized rather than balanced
- Weather events (storms, tidal surges) directly affect combat outcomes
- Air superiority matters for inter-island power projection
- Transport capability is the most strategically valuable asset
