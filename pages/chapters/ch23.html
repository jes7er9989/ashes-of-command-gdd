THE WAR COUNCIL & ADVISORS
15 Unique Characters (5 Factions × 3) · Doctrine Recommendations · Hidden Depths — Chapter 23
Convenes automatically: every 5 cycles, before Approach Phase, after major events (Vorax arrival, faction elimination, Guardian contact), and on loyalty warnings. Three advisors provide recommendations colored by their doctrine. Player listens, then decides. Every reaction is tracked. Nothing is neutral.

The Three Advisors
Valerius · Krell · Vane — Three Doctrines, Three Hidden Depths
MAJOR VALERIUS — THE ARCHITECT
Calm, measured, speaks in probabilities and projections. Advocates patience, infrastructure, intelligence. At Approach Phase: always recommends Espionage first, Diplomacy second, War only as last resort.
Hidden Depth: Has a family on a sector he has mathematically calculated as "acceptable losses." His heart tells him otherwise. He never mentions it unless the player asks him directly — and even then, he deflects once before admitting. In the Terran Architect-aligned path, the Wake-Up recommendation during Final War comes from him — the canonical default.
GENERAL KRELL — THE VANGUARD
Rough, direct, speaks in absolutes and battlefield idioms. Advocates immediate aggression, overwhelming force, minimum hesitation. At Approach Phase: recommends War every time, without exception. Occasionally respected by enemy Generals.
Hidden Depth: Has an unbroken win record across hundreds of engagements. But the kill count accumulates behind his eyes. He advocates war not because he believes in it — it is simply the only language he knows how to speak. Rarely, very late in the campaign, he asks: "Was any of it worth it?"
INQUISITOR VANE — THE TYRANT
Quiet, deliberate, speaks entirely in implications and questions that are really statements. Advocates espionage, manufactured fear, population control, strategic cruelty. At Approach Phase: recommends Sabotage. Always.
Hidden Depth: Was an idealist. Brilliant, naive, full of hope. A single crisis event early in her career broke something that never healed. Not evil — TERRIFIED. Believes cruelty is the only effective deterrent in a universe that punishes mercy. The most dangerous person on the command deck — because she is not wrong about the galaxy, only about herself.

Simulation → Final War Reveal
The Twist That Recontextualizes Everything
During the simulation, advisors feel like AI constructs — program voices, support functions. The player assumes they are algorithms. Their recommendations feel like generated output. Their secrets feel like backstory flavor.
In the Final War: they are real people. Faces. Physical presence. Trembling hands. Personal stakes. Everything they said throughout the campaign was sincere — not playing roles, but teaching. Preparing. Warning. The player now understands every piece of advice they ignored.
Estimated dialogue: 50–80 lines each during simulation · 100–150 lines each during Final War. The Wake-Up recommendation is delivered by whichever advisor has the best relationship with the Commander — earned, not scripted. Valerius is the canonical/trailer default (Terran Architect-aligned).

Advisor Inter-Relationships
The Triangle That Shifts With Alignment
The three advisors were selected/assigned TOGETHER — they have history before the Commander existed. They argue, defend, and undermine each other based on which doctrine the Commander favors. This makes the war council feel ALIVE.
ARCHITECT ↔ VANGUARD
Grudging mutual respect. Disagree on everything but recognize competence.
ARCHITECT ↔ TYRANT
Intellectual tension. Both strategic thinkers — one builds, the other breaks.
VANGUARD ↔ TYRANT
Most friction. The soldier despises manipulation. The manipulator sees a blunt instrument.

The Wake-Up Moment
The Most Important Narrative Beat — Earned, Not Scripted
“Leave the simulation. The real war is here.” — delivered by whichever advisor has the best relationship with the Commander.
Tyrant advisor: “This training ground has taught you everything it can. The real galaxy is burning. You know what must be done.”
Vanguard advisor: “We’ve run enough drills. People are dying out there. REAL people. It’s time.”
Architect advisor: “The metrics suggest readiness. But readiness isn’t the point anymore. They need you.”
Balanced path: ALL THREE deliver it together. Rare moment of unity — three people who disagree on everything, agreeing on this one thing.
Advisor Death
The Sidelined Advisor Is At Risk
The advisor you ignored most takes a desperate risk in the Final War to prove they matter.
Some Relationship Maintained: They survive. Redemption moment. They earn their place.
Completely Neglected: They die. Underprepared, desperate, trying to matter. Your NEGLECT killed them, not the enemy.
Aftermath: Favored advisor feels guilt (“I should have included them”). Middle advisor feels anger (“You never listened to them. Not once.”). Hidden depth revealed posthumously.
Balanced/Unifier path is safest for keeping all three alive. Tyrant path is most dangerous — Architect advisor almost always at risk.
Advisors & The Strategist
Gradual Awareness — They Can’t Hear It, But They See the Changes
Early: Commander acts on hunches. Advisors chalk it up to intuition. “Good instinct, Commander.”
Mid: Commander references impossible intelligence. Pauses, listening to something invisible. Advisors notice.
Architect: “Your sources are unusually thorough. Where is this intelligence coming from?”
Vanguard: “Whatever’s in your head, keep it together.”
Tyrant: “Something is influencing you. I need to know what. Now.”
Late: Undeniable. Each responds per doctrine — Architect fascinated, Vanguard tolerant (“if it helps us win”), Tyrant threatened (another voice is competition).
Final War: All three have a relationship with the Strategist by proxy. The Strategist has opinions about THEM too — shares only if asked.
ADVISORS & THE AETHYN TRUTH — SHARED REVELATION
They learn WITH the Commander — no information asymmetry. Each processes through their doctrine lens:
Architect: “If all factions share a common ancestor… alliances aren’t just strategic. They’re reunification.”
Vanguard: “Doesn’t matter where we came from. What matters is who’s threatening us NOW.”
Tyrant: “If all factions are fragments of one species… whoever controls the Engine controls the WHOLE species.”
