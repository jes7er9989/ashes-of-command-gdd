GROUND COMBAT VISION
Dark Crusade + StarCraft + Mechabellum — The Blend
§19b.1 — Design Inspirations
Three Games · One System
DAWN OF WAR: DARK CRUSADE
Territory conquest map. Garrison defense. Attacker deploys from map edge. Buildings give defensive bonuses. Every territory is a place with meaning, not just a hex. The weight and permanence of conquest.
STARCRAFT
Unit composition and counter-play. Rock-paper-scissors dynamics. Positioning matters. The satisfaction of building the right army for the right fight. Visual readability at a glance.
MECHABELLUM
Auto-battler deployment. Formation strategy. Reserve waves. Player sets up, then watches with surgical intervention. The commander fantasy — preparation IS the skill expression.
§19b.2 — The Blend
How Each Contributes to Ground Combat
Pre-Battle: Deploy units in formation (Mechabellum) at a territory (Dark Crusade)
During Battle: Units auto-fight (Mechabellum) with counter-play mattering (StarCraft)
Terrain: Defender has building bonuses from territory (Dark Crusade)
Decisions: Player picks composition + formation + reserves, then watches. CP abilities for surgical intervention.
NON-NEGOTIABLE: AUTO-BATTLER STAYS
The auto-battler core is the foundation of the entire combat experience. Players do NOT directly control units. The skill expression is in preparation (80%) and intervention (20%). This is what makes Ashes of Command a strategy game, not a tactics game.
§19b.3 — Missing Pieces to Build
Systems Not Yet Implemented
1. Terrain & Cover
Units behind obstacles take less damage. Cover positions matter for deployment.
2. Defender Garrison Bonus
Buildings provide stat bonuses to defending units. Makes territory development directly impact combat.
3. Strategic Capture Points
Key points to capture during battle for tactical advantage. Encourages dynamic movement.
4. Flanking Bonus
Side/rear attacks deal more damage. Rewards positioning and formation depth.
