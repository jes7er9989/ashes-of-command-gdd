Identity & Lore
The Price of Order
The Unity Accord is the galaxy's most organized civilization — a multi-species coalition unified under a rigid caste system, governed by a council of elected representatives, and protected by the most technologically sophisticated conventional military in existence. They are not a single species but a COALITION: dozens of alien races that voluntarily (or semi-voluntarily) submitted to the Accord's governance structure in exchange for protection, economic integration, and advanced technology.
The Accord believes in a single foundational principle: the galaxy's greatest threat is not any specific enemy but CHAOS ITSELF — the entropy of competing civilizations and uncoordinated responses that allowed the original civilization to fragment and fall. The Accord's answer is ORDER. Absolute, comprehensive, non-negotiable. Every citizen has a caste. Every caste has a function. Every function serves the greater whole.
Their military reflects this philosophy: long-range plasma weaponry that eliminates threats before they close, sophisticated drone networks extending surveillance, and a Tactical Link system sharing targeting data in real-time. The Accord does not fight with heroes. It fights with SYSTEMS. Their greatest strength is coordination. Their greatest weakness is rigidity — when circumstances deviate from the plan, the caste system suppresses the individual initiative needed to adapt.

Wedge-shaped with smooth featureless hulls. The most "modern" ships — designed by engineers, not artists. Internal warm amber lighting. Polished white-and-gold composite. Every design decision optimal. Whether that answer has SOUL is a separate question.
Gameplay
Primary Mechanic: Marked Targets (Tactical Link)
ONE HIT MARKS THE TARGET FOR THE ENTIRE ARMY
When any Accord unit hits an enemy, the target's data is broadcast to every other Accord unit. All subsequent shots gain +15% accuracy. Multiple targets can be Marked simultaneously. The entire army converges fire with preternatural precision.
Mark Bonus: +15% accuracy (auto), +20% with Commander aura
Marker Light: +15% additional (total +30% combined)
Duration: 8 seconds after last hit (expires if not refreshed)
Multiple Marks: Dozens of targets simultaneously in large battles
Accuracy Only: Does not increase damage, only hit chance
Counter: Mobility, dispersion, break line-of-sight
Worst Matchup: Scrap-Horde swarms dilute focus-fire
