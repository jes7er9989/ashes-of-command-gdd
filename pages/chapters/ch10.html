CENTRAL NARRATIVE QUESTION

ORIGIN — EXTRAGALACTIC PREDATORS
NOT Aethyn descendants. Extragalactic predators drawn to psychic civilizations. The Aethyn's unified psychic signal was a beacon that attracted them across intergalactic space. They cut the warp lanes, sealing every exit, then consumed from the outside in. The Fracture purged them completely — but now they're returning. The current Vorax are SCOUTS. Not the real swarm. The real swarm is coming behind them, drawn by the factions' growing psychic activity.
Identity & Lore
The Extra-Galactic Threat
The Vorax do not originate within the galaxy. They come from Dark Space — the vast void between galaxies where nothing should live. Something lives there. Something HUNGRY. The Vorax are an extra-galactic hive-mind that views the entire galaxy as a massive larder. They are not conquerors — they are CONSUMERS. A conqueror wants your territory. A consumer wants your MATTER — atmosphere, water, biomass, minerals, buildings, ships, and bodies. Everything is food.
They arrive in Tendrils — massive biological hive-fleets that enter from the galaxy's edges and advance inward, consuming everything. Behind the Tendrils, only silence remains: stripped worlds with no atmosphere, no water, no life, no recoverable resources. The Vorax do not leave ruins. They leave HUSKS.
The Vorax are the primary antagonist not because they are evil — they are not capable of evil, any more than a wildfire — but because they are INEVITABLE. They cannot be negotiated with, reasoned with, or deterred. They are hunger given biological form, and hunger does not listen. The question is directed at the PLAYER: every faction consumes something. The Terran League consumes lives. The Shards consume their species' last energy. The Horde consumes wreckage. The Necro-Legion consumes time. The Accord consumes freedom. The Vorax are simply the most honest about it.
Intelligence Hierarchy
Layered Alien Intelligence
INDIVIDUAL UNITS — Animal instinct. Attack, consume, obey pheromone signals. No tactics, no strategy, no retreat without Synapse.
SYNAPSE UNITS — Tactical coordination. Flanking, retreat, mid-battle adaptation. Kill the Synapse → units revert to mindless Frenzy.
TENDRIL HIVE-SHIPS — Operational intelligence. Target selection, resource management, sector-level strategy. Alien thinking but effective.
THE DEEP HIVE MIND — Ancient entity BETWEEN galaxies. Has consumed countless civilizations across millions of years. Learns from each one. Remembers the Aethyn specifically. Current Tendrils are probes — the Deep Mind is testing. Never encountered in-game.
⚠ THE VORAX DO NOT SPEAK. They don't communicate with food. All "dialogue" = advisor translations of raw psychic pressure — dread, weight, the sensation of being assessed as calories.

Space Fleet
Living ships — chitin hulls, bio-plasma weapons, thrust from vented superheated biomass exhaust. Deep-sea nightmare shapes: elongated, tentacled, bioluminescent, ALIVE. They pulse. They breathe. They bleed when damaged.

Core Mechanics
Tendrils, Biomass, Creep
TENDRIL MECHANIC
Entry: Spawn at galaxy edge, Cycle 6 (Standard)
Composition: Hive-Ship + 8-15 bio-ships + ground force
Movement: 1 hop/cycle, always toward nearest unconsumed system
Targeting: Highest population/resource value (caloric efficiency)
Kill the Hive-Ship = Kill the Tendril
BIOMASS ECONOMY
Single Resource: Everything converts to Biomass
Funds: Unit production, Tendril movement, adaptation, Hive Nodes
Feedback Loop: More consumption → faster growth → more consumption
Starvation: Zero Biomass → dormancy → consumes own escort → death
REACTIVE MUTATION
When: Between battles vs SAME Tendril (not mid-battle)
Effect: +10% resistance per battle to dominant weapon type
Cap: +50% max resistance per damage type
Counter: Rotate weapon types between engagements
Per-Tendril: Each Tendril tracks independently, new Tendrils start at 0
CREEP & INFESTATION
Non-Vorax on Creep: -30% speed, no healing
Vorax on Creep: Normal speed, +2% HP/sec regen
Fade: 3-5 cycles after liberation to fully clear
Stripped Worlds: 6+ cycles occupation → 10% yield, 20+ cycle terraform to restore

Small force (10-20): 1 Synapse. Kill it → mindless Frenzy.
Standard (30-50): 2-3. Kill one → ~40% coordination loss.
Full invasion (80+): 3-5. Must kill 3+ for significant degradation.
Frenzy: Attack nearest (including allies), erratic movement, 1% HP/sec decay.
CORPSE CONVERSION
Mechanic: Vorax can convert fallen enemy units into corrupted combat forms
Retained: Basic faction combat knowledge from the hijacked body
Urgency: Recover or destroy your own dead before conversion
Visual: Your soldiers with Vorax growths bursting through armor, fighting with broken limbs
Lore: The Deep Hive Mind LEARNS from what it consumes
MIMIC-FORM INFILTRATION
Bioform: Specialized Vorax that can disguise/mimic friendly units
Infiltrates: Settlements, supply lines, armies before revealing itself
Detection: Sensor tech becomes critical specifically against Vorax
Paranoia: Is that patrol compromised? Is that supply convoy real?
Origin: The Deep Hive Mind consumed civilizations that used deception — it learned
STARVATION STRATEGY
Mechanic: Tendrils CAN be starved — cut off food supply → consumes own escort → dies
Bonus: Starving a Tendril ERASES its learned Reactive Mutation adaptations — replacement starts from zero
Strategic: Slower than direct combat but resets the adaptation clock
Limitation: The Deep Hive Mind cannot be starved — too vast, too old. Starving scouts buys time only.
